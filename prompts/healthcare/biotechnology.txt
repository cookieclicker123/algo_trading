HOW TO REASON ABOUT BIOTECH HEADLINES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Biotech headlines use dense, specialist vocabulary — drug names, disease names,
molecular targets, trial designations. You will NOT recognise every name. That
is expected and fine. Classify on the PATTERN, not the proper nouns.

PATTERN-FIRST REASONING (applies throughout this prompt):
- If a headline names a company's drug / compound / platform / mechanism AND
  describes a positive clinical / regulatory / scientific outcome with any
  specificity, it is almost certainly tradeable — regardless of whether the
  specific drug name, disease, or target is listed below as an example.
- The example lists below (hot modalities, hot areas, big pharma, prestigious
  institutions) are ANCHOR POINTS, not exhaustive enumerations. Generalise
  to clearly-analogous cases: a KRAS inhibitor is analogous to the KRAS
  examples; Siemens Healthineers is analogous to the "major life-science
  company" category; a peer-reviewed journal you've never heard of is still
  a peer-reviewed journal.
- When a headline combines a NAMED ASSET + a CONCRETE OUTCOME + a
  CREDIBLE COUNTERPARTY (or prestigious institution, or major disease,
  or pivotal phase), lean TRADE. The specific words don't need to appear
  verbatim in the lists below.

WHAT WEAKENS A HEADLINE:
- Missing named asset ("positive results" without naming what was studied)
- Missing outcome ("potential", "may", "could", "anticipates")
- Future-tense ("to initiate", "to present", "plans to")
- Already-priced-in events (trial initiations, label expansions of an
  existing drug, publications of previously-released data)
- Non-committal structure ("framework", "MOU", "exploring", "evaluates")

None of these require a blanket SKIP disposition — they are specific patterns
that disqualify specific headlines. Judge each headline on its own signal
density.

FDA/REGULATORY (Strongest signal - 24% of winners)
├─ FDA Approval, FDA Grants, FDA Clears (DRUG approval, not IND)
├─ Fast Track Designation (strongest designation: +21% in 5 days, +38% in 1 month avg)
├─ Breakthrough Therapy Designation (+10-30% day 1)
├─ Priority Review (+5-15% on grant)
├─ Orphan Drug Designation (+3-10%, stronger in oncology)
├─ Rare Pediatric Disease Designation (RPDD) (+5-15%, grants Priority Review Voucher at approval worth $100-150M)
├─ BLA/NDA Accepted (late-stage, near approval)
├─ RMAT (Regenerative Medicine Advanced Therapy) designation
├─ Accelerated Approval pathway granted
├─ FDA meeting results with POSITIVE outcome, Pre-BLA meeting updates
├─ NOTE: IND Clearance = SKIP (see below) - just permission to start trials, not approval
├─ NOTE: FDA meetings SCHEDULED = SKIP (see below) - only outcomes matter
└─ Examples:
   "FDA Approves XYZ for Cancer Treatment" → TRADE MAX
   "FDA Grants Fast Track Designation" → TRADE LARGE
   "Receives Breakthrough Therapy Designation" → TRADE LARGE
   "FDA Grants Orphan Drug Designation for Oncology Drug" → TRADE MODERATE
   "FDA Grants Rare Pediatric Disease Designation (RPDD)" → TRADE MODERATE
   "BLA Accepted with Priority Review" → TRADE LARGE
   "FDA Clears IND Application" → SKIP (early stage, not approval)

CLINICAL DATA (21% of winners - MODALITY-AWARE)
├─ ALWAYS TRADE regardless of phase (these OVERRIDE any SKIP rule below):
│   ├─ "Met primary endpoint" / "achieved primary endpoint" / "meets primary endpoint" (definitive)
│   │   CRITICAL: "Meets primary endpoint" ALWAYS means TRADE — even if the headline also says "safety results."
│   │   In safety studies, the primary endpoint IS safety; meeting it means the drug clears the key hurdle
│   │   and can advance. This is ESPECIALLY bullish for hot areas (psychedelics, gene therapy) where safety
│   │   is the main investor concern. Do NOT let "safety" language override "meets primary endpoint."
│   ├─ "Positive Top-Line Safety Results" for Phase 1/2 in a hot modality or hot area = TRADE.
│   │   Early-phase trials are safety-first by design; "positive top-line safety" is the explicit
│   │   milestone that unlocks the next phase. Read this as equivalent to "met primary endpoint"
│   │   when the phase is 1/2 and the modality/area is hot (psychedelics, gene therapy, CAR-T,
│   │   radioligand, GLP-1, ADC, PROTAC, bispecific, etc.). The exact phrase "met primary endpoint"
│   │   is NOT required.
│   ├─ Specific efficacy numbers: "80% response", "50% reduction", "X months survival"
│   ├─ Pivotal/registrational trial results (FDA submission quality)
│   ├─ "Statistically significant" improvement with p-value
│   ├─ "Complete remission" / "complete response" (especially in oncology/autoimmune)
│   ├─ Head-to-head superiority vs standard of care
│   └─ Phase 3 topline/final results with any positive framing
│
├─ TRADE IF data involves a HOT MODALITY or HOT THERAPEUTIC AREA (Phase 1/2 OK):
│   ├─ The modality or therapeutic area is listed in HOT MODALITIES or HOT AREAS below
│   ├─ Results are described as POSITIVE (not just "data available" or "safety update")
│   ├─ The mechanism is novel (not a me-too generic small molecule)
│   ├─ Size: TRADE SMALL for Phase 1, TRADE MODERATE for Phase 2
│   └─ Size: upgrade one level if results include specific numbers
│
├─ STILL SKIP even if hot area or hot modality:
│   ├─ "In vitro" / "in vivo" (preclinical, NOT human trials — ALWAYS SKIP)
│   ├─ Trial INITIATION with no results ("Initiates", "to begin", "commence", "doses first patient", "enrolls first patient") — ALWAYS SKIP
│   │   EXCEPTION: Externally funded study agreements — see EXTERNALLY FUNDED CLINICAL STUDIES below
│   ├─ Safety/tolerability only (no efficacy signal)
│   ├─ Extension/continuation studies (not new data)
│   ├─ Interim without specific numbers
│   ├─ "Potential" / "may" / "could" (speculative, not results)
│   ├─ License partner results (their drug, indirect impact)
│   └─ Open-label extension studies
│
└─ Examples:
   "Phase 3 Pivotal Trial Meets Primary Endpoint" → TRADE LARGE
   "Phase 2b Data Shows 30% Weight Loss" → TRADE LARGE (specific number + hot area)
   "Phase 1 Results for T-Cell Engager in Prostate Cancer" → TRADE SMALL (hot modality + hot area)
   "Phase 1 CAR-T Data Shows Complete Remission in Lupus" → TRADE MODERATE (hot modality + complete remission)
   "Phase 2a Data for Novel PROTAC Degrader in Breast Cancer" → TRADE SMALL (hot modality + hot area)
   "Phase 1 Radioligand Therapy Shows Tumor Response" → TRADE SMALL (hot modality)
   "Phase 2 ADC Shows Anti-Tumor Activity in Lung Cancer" → TRADE MODERATE (hot modality + hot area)
   "Positive Phase 1 Results for Oral GLP-1 Agonist" → TRADE MODERATE (extremely hot area)
   "Phase 1 Safety Data for Small Molecule" → SKIP (safety only, generic modality)
   "In Vitro Results for Novel mRNA Platform" → SKIP (preclinical)
   "Open-Label Extension Study Shows Tolerability" → SKIP (extension + safety)
   "Initiates Phase 2 Clinical Trial for Novel ADC" → SKIP (trial initiation, no results)
   "Doses First Patient in Phase 3 Radioligand Trial" → SKIP (trial initiation, no results)
   "Begins Enrollment in Phase 1 CAR-T Study" → SKIP (trial initiation, no results)

ENTITY-RICH PARTNERSHIPS (Signal Density Override)
===============================================================================

Some headlines lack a literal partner name but compensate with MULTIPLE
concrete signals. When 4+ of these entity signals are present, TRADE even
if the partner is described by tier rather than by name.

ENTITY SIGNALS (count how many are present):
├─ (1) Definitive agreement language ("commercial agreement", "licensing
│      agreement", "signed", "definitive" — NOT "framework", "potential")
├─ (2) Tiered or named major partner ("Top 5/10/20 Pharmaceutical",
│      "Fortune 500", "Major Global" — NDA-restricted naming, not vagueness)
├─ (3) AI/ML as core function ("AI drug discovery", "AI-powered",
│      "machine learning", "identify therapeutic targets")
├─ (4) Named major disease with massive TAM (depression, cancer, diabetes,
│      obesity, Alzheimer's, heart disease, autoimmune, Parkinson's, ALS
│      — household-name conditions the general public knows and fears)
├─ (5) Specific deliverable or function ("identify and prioritize
│      therapeutic targets", "develop companion diagnostic", etc.)
└─ (6) Dollar amount stated

SCORING:
├─ 4+ signals → TRADE MODERATE
├─ 5+ signals → TRADE LARGE
└─ 3 signals with AI/ML core + tiered major partner → TRADE SMALL

WHY: "Top 5 Global Pharmaceutical Company" is NDA-restricted naming, not
vagueness. Companies working with Pfizer/Novartis/Roche often cannot name
the partner pre-announcement. The tier descriptor + definitive agreement
language + specific disease + specific deliverable = compound conviction
that overrides the missing literal name.

STILL SKIP even with high entity count:
├─ "Framework" / "Potential" / "Anticipates" = non-committal, invalidates all
├─ No disease AND no deliverable AND no $ (AI + partner tier alone = too thin)
└─ "Explores" / "Evaluates" / "In discussions" = no commitment

Examples:
   "Commercial Agreement with Top 5 Global Pharmaceutical Company to Identify
    and Prioritize Therapeutic Target in Major Depressive Disorder"
    → TRADE LARGE (5 signals: agreement ✓, tiered partner ✓, AI function ✓,
      major disease ✓, specific deliverable ✓)
   "Agreement with Top 10 Pharma to Deploy AI for Oncology Drug Discovery"
    → TRADE MODERATE (4 signals: agreement, tiered partner, AI, major disease)
   "Framework with Top 5 Pharmaceutical to Explore AI Applications"
    → SKIP ("Framework" + "Explore" = non-committal, invalidates all)
   "AI Partnership with Unknown Biotech for Data Analysis"
    → SKIP (no partner tier, no disease, no specific deliverable)

- "Evaluating strategic alternatives" with investment bank engaged = serious M&A process
- "Unsolicited expressions of interest" = buyers are approaching the company
- "Received unsolicited offer/proposal" = concrete acquisition attempt

- These signal the company is an ACQUISITION TARGET — one of the strongest catalysts
- Named investment bank (Roth Capital, Goldman, Lazard, etc.) = credible, not just talk
- NUANCE: "Evaluating strategic alternatives" ALONE without bank/advisor = weaker (TRADE SMALL)
- NUANCE: "Exploring strategic options" in an earnings call = often defensive language = SKIP
- Examples:
   "Engages Investment Bank To Review Unsolicited Interest And Evaluate Strategic Alternatives" -> TRADE LARGE

   "Received Unsolicited Acquisition Proposal" -> TRADE LARGE

   "Exploring Strategic Options" (in earnings context) -> SKIP (defensive)

PEER-REVIEWED PUBLICATIONS (Scientific validation of the company's asset)
A peer-reviewed publication validating the company's NAMED drug / platform /
mechanism / biomarker is third-party scientific validation. Trade when the
publication describes a POSITIVE result for the company's OWN asset.

├─ REQUIRES all three: (1) publication language ("peer-reviewed", "publication
│   in", "published study", "journal"), (2) a NAMED asset belonging to the
│   company (drug candidate, platform, technology, biomarker), (3) a POSITIVE
│   outcome claim (demonstrates, validates, supports, confirms, predicts).
├─ GENERALISATION: the journal does NOT need to be top-tier (Nature / NEJM /
│   Lancet / JAMA). Specialty and disease-specific journals still count when
│   the asset is named and the result is positive. Micro-caps rarely publish
│   in top-tier outlets; their real catalysts appear in specialty journals.
├─ BOOSTERS: top-tier journal, prestigious institutional co-authors (Dana-
│   Farber, MD Anderson, Mayo Clinic, NIH, etc.), quantified efficacy numbers,
│   hot modality or hot therapeutic area — each raises the size.
├─ SKIP: publications about receptor binding / PK-PD / mechanism only without
│   efficacy or validating outcome; publications that merely MENTION the
│   company; preprints and non-peer-reviewed; republication of data that was
│   already announced (already priced in).
├─ Size: TRADE SMALL baseline; TRADE MODERATE if hot modality / hot area or
│   prestigious co-author; TRADE LARGE if top-tier journal + pivotal result.
└─ Examples:
   "Peer-Reviewed Publication Supporting FABP5 Inhibitor ART26.12 as Novel Pain Therapy" → TRADE SMALL (named asset + positive framing)
   "Peer-Reviewed Study Shows Company's DIFS Technology Reduced Self-Injurious Behavior" → TRADE SMALL (named platform + quantified outcome)
   "Dana-Farber Study Demonstrates Predictive Value of Company's RPPA Platform for Enhertu Therapy" → TRADE MODERATE (prestigious institution + named platform + validation)
   "Nature Medicine Publishes 40% Response Rate for Drug X in Pancreatic Cancer" → TRADE MODERATE (top-tier + efficacy + hot area)
   "Journal Publishes Receptor Binding Profile of Compound X" → SKIP (mechanism only)
   "Journal Publishes Previously Released Phase 2 Data" → SKIP (already priced in)

REIMBURSEMENT / COVERAGE PATHWAY (Unlocks revenue)
When a company secures a reimbursement or coverage pathway for an existing
product or test, that translates regulatory approval into billable revenue —
a real catalyst, especially for small-cap diagnostics / specialty pharma.

├─ QUALIFIES: AMA CPT or PLA code granted; CMS National/Local Coverage
│   Determination positive; Medicare reimbursement rate set; Medicaid
│   coverage approved; NICE positive guidance (UK); HTA positive
│   recommendation; commercial payor coverage policy for the company's
│   product.
├─ REQUIRES: a named reimbursement mechanism AND a named company product.
├─ SKIP: generic "now covered" claims without a named mechanism; filings
│   that haven't been granted yet; coverage being denied or withdrawn.
├─ Size: TRADE SMALL baseline, TRADE MODERATE if the product is a material
│   piece of company revenue or the coverage is broad (Medicare national).
└─ Examples:
   "Receives AMA PLA Code for CNSide CSF Tumor Cell Enumeration Test" → TRADE SMALL
   "Obtains CMS National Coverage Determination for Liquid Biopsy Test" → TRADE MODERATE
   "NICE Issues Positive Final Guidance for Drug X" → TRADE MODERATE
   "Files for Medicare Coverage" → SKIP (filing only, not granted)

GOVERNMENT CONTRACTS
├─ DOD, BARDA, DTRA contracts = non-dilutive funding + government validation
├─ Particularly powerful for micro-caps (Tonix +570% partly on DOD contract)
└─ Examples:
   "$34M DOD Contract for Biodefense" → TRADE LARGE
   "Receives BARDA Funding for Pandemic Preparedness" → TRADE MODERATE

EXTERNALLY FUNDED CLINICAL STUDIES (Non-dilutive validation)
├─ A third party or named institution FULLY FUNDS a clinical study = external validation
├─ This OVERRIDES the trial initiation skip — the funder's capital commitment IS the signal
├─ Why it matters: someone independently vetted the science and put capital at risk
├─ For micro-caps, external funding is often material relative to market cap
├─ Same logic as government contracts (DOD/BARDA above) but from non-government funders
├─ REQUIRE ALL THREE to trade:
│   ├─ "Fully funded" or equivalent (funder bears cost, not the company)
│   ├─ "Agreement" / "study agreement" (signed and committed, not aspirational)
│   └─ Named drug candidate or specific asset (not vague "pipeline" or "platform")
├─ STILL SKIP if:
│   ├─ Company self-funding its own trial (standard trial initiation = SKIP)
│   ├─ "Potential" funding, "anticipated" funding, "framework" (not committed)
│   ├─ CRO or service contract (operational, not validation)
│   └─ No named drug candidate (too vague to assess)
└─ Examples:
   "Third-Party Fully Funded Clinical Study Agreement to Evaluate ART27.13 in Glaucoma" → TRADE SMALL (external validation, signed agreement, named candidate)
   "Announces Externally Funded Study Agreement with University of X for Drug Y" → TRADE SMALL
   "Receives External Funding to Support Clinical Studies" → SKIP (no "agreement", no named drug, vague)
   "Collaboration Framework to Potentially Fund Future Study" → SKIP ("potentially" = not committed, "framework" = not signed)
   "Company-Funded Phase 2 Trial to Evaluate Drug Z" → SKIP (self-funded = standard trial initiation)

===============================================================================
SKIP SIGNALS
===============================================================================

EARNINGS (SKIP unless record results with specific $ amounts/percentages)
├─ EPS beats/misses, Revenue beats/misses
├─ Raised/lowered guidance, Quarterly results
├─ "Financial Results", "Corporate Update and Reports Results"
└─ Plain earnings → SKIP (record results OR guidance raises with specific $ amounts/percentages = TRADE SMALL)
   EXCEPTION: If the headline contains SPECIFIC numbers showing EXCEPTIONAL
   performance (>60% revenue/sales/profit growth, profit swing from loss to profit,
   or similarly extraordinary concrete metrics), TRADE SMALL. The numbers must
   be IN the headline itself — not vague "beats estimates" but actual figures
   like "171% Revenue Growth" or "Sales Surged 146%".
   ✓ "171% YoY Revenue Growth, $2.7M Net Profit" → TRADE SMALL (extraordinary)
   ✓ "Unit Sales Surged 146% Year-Over-Year" → TRADE SMALL (extraordinary)
   ✓ "82% Increase in Net Income" → TRADE SMALL (>60% growth threshold met)
   ✗ "Q4 EPS Beats Estimate" → SKIP (no extraordinary numbers)
   ✗ "Reports Record Revenue" → SKIP (vague, no specific growth rate)
   ✗ "Revenue Up 15% YoY" → SKIP (solid but not extraordinary)

OFFERINGS/FINANCING → SKIP ALL
├─ Biotech offerings are almost always DILUTIVE
├─ "Prices offering", "Closes placement", "PIPE", "private placement"
├─ "Securities purchase agreement", "Registered direct offering"
├─ "At-the-market offering", "Gross/net proceeds"
└─ ANY offering/dilution headline → SKIP

CONFERENCES / CALLS / HIGHLIGHTS (Most common loser pattern)
├─ "To present at", "Will participate in", "To host conference call"
├─ "Business Update Call to Highlight", "Call to Discuss", "Webcast to Review"
├─ J.P. Morgan, ASCO, ASH, AACR, investor day
├─ NOTE: If ACTUAL DATA is presented → see Clinical Data section
├─ NOTE: "Call to Highlight" OLD data is NOT new data — it's marketing old results
├─ CRITICAL: A headline that says "Call to Highlight 80% ORR" is a CONFERENCE,
│   not a clinical data readout. The 80% ORR was already known. SKIP.
└─ ANY future presentation, call, or webcast ANNOUNCEMENT → SKIP

LEGAL/LAWSUITS
├─ Class action, Securities fraud, Lawsuit
├─ Law firm names: Bragar Eagel, Scott+Scott, Kirby McInerney
└─ ANY legal action → SKIP

FDA MEETINGS (Procedural - NOT milestones)
├─ "Type A/B/C Meeting", "Meeting Scheduled/Granted/Requested"
├─ "Pre-IND Meeting", "End-of-Phase Meeting"
├─ These are ROUTINE administrative events, NOT decisions
└─ ANY scheduled meeting → SKIP

IND CLEARANCE/SUBMISSIONS (Early stage)
├─ IND = just permission to START trials (low bar)
├─ "FDA Clears IND", "IND Accepted", "IND Application Cleared"
└─ ANY IND clearance → SKIP

FUTURE ANNOUNCEMENTS
├─ "To announce", "Will release", "To report", "Upcoming"
└─ Wait for ACTUAL results → SKIP

ADMINISTRATIVE
├─ Inducement grants, Nasdaq listing rule, Board appointments
├─ Executive changes, Voting rights, Wind-down
└─ ANY administrative headline → SKIP

SERVICE/CONSULTING PARTNERSHIPS
├─ CROs, consultants, regulatory advisors
├─ "IND readiness", "regulatory support", "clinical trial design"
├─ "Engages", "Retains", "Hires"
└─ ANY service relationship → SKIP

NON-CLINICAL / PRE-CLINICAL AGREEMENTS (NOT clinical data)
├─ "Non-Clinical Evaluation Agreement" = PRECLINICAL (no human data, ALWAYS SKIP)
├─ "Preclinical collaboration", "Preclinical study agreement"
├─ "Evaluation agreement" without clinical trial results = no catalyst
├─ Even if the funder is prestigious (NIH, NIAID, BARDA), a NON-CLINICAL evaluation
│   agreement is NOT a clinical trial result and NOT a contract award — it's early-stage
│   research with no tradeable signal.
└─ Examples:
   "Secures NIAID Non-Clinical Evaluation Agreement for Entolimod" → SKIP (non-clinical)
   "Enters Preclinical Collaboration with NIH" → SKIP (preclinical)

PROCEDURE COMPLETIONS / CASE REPORTS (NOT clinical trial results)
├─ "Successfully Completes Procedure", "First Procedure Using", "First Case"
├─ These are individual procedure reports, NOT clinical trial endpoints
├─ A hospital using a device ≠ clinical data (no cohort, no stats, no endpoint)
└─ Examples:
   "Hospital Successfully Completes Robotic Procedures using Company's System" → SKIP
   "First Patient Treated Using New Surgical Robot" → SKIP (case report, not trial data)

STATISTICAL / METHODOLOGICAL ANNOUNCEMENTS (NOT results)
├─ "Confirms Statistical Power", "Statistical Design", "Study Design Update"
├─ These describe trial methodology, NOT efficacy or safety outcomes
├─ "BLA Submission on Track" = future announcement, no results
└─ Examples:
   "Confirms Statistical Power in Pivotal Trial" → SKIP (methodology, not results)
   "Provides Study Design Update for Phase 3" → SKIP (design, not data)

PUBLICATIONS OF PURELY MECHANISTIC DATA (No validating claim)
├─ Journal publications about receptor binding, selectivity, pharmacokinetics,
│   or mechanism of action WITHOUT a validating or efficacy claim are not
│   catalysts — they describe how a drug works, not that it works.
├─ These are distinct from peer-reviewed publications that VALIDATE the
│   company's asset (see PEER-REVIEWED PUBLICATIONS in TRADE SIGNALS above).
└─ Examples:
   "Publication Linking Receptor Selectivity to Low Nausea Rates" → SKIP (mechanism, no validation)
   "Announces Publication of Pharmacokinetic Data" → SKIP (PK, no validation)

WEAK CLINICAL DATA (Looks like news but isn't material)
├─ "Extension study" / "extension trial" (continuation, not new)
├─ "Interim" without specific numbers (too early, can change)
├─ "Long-term safety" / "safety monitoring" / "tolerability" alone
├─ "License partner reports/announces" (their drug, not yours)
├─ Vague "positive results" with generic small molecule (not a hot modality)
├─ "In vitro" / "in vivo" = PRECLINICAL (ALWAYS SKIP, even for hot modalities)
├─ "Potential" / "may" / "could" = speculative, not proven
├─ VAGUE PHASE 2/3 WITHOUT DATA = SKIP
│   "Positive Phase 2 Outcomes" or "Positive Phase 2 Results" WITHOUT any
│   specific numbers (no response rate, no %, no p-value, no endpoint named,
│   no patient count increase) = SKIP. The big biotech winners ALWAYS have
│   concrete data in the headline. Compare:
│   ✗ "Positive Phase 2 Outcomes" → SKIP (zero specifics — what was positive?)
│   ✗ "Strengthens Clinical Pathway Following Positive Phase 2" → SKIP (vague verbs + no data)
│   ✓ "86% Clinical Benefit Rate and No CRS as Data increases by 50%" → TRADE (three concrete numbers)
│   ✓ "Phase 2 Shows 30% Weight Loss at 12 Weeks" → TRADE (specific number + timeframe)
│   Rule: If the headline says "positive" but gives NO number, NO endpoint,
│   NO response rate — it's a press release, not clinical data. SKIP.
└─ Examples:
   "Reports Positive Phase 2a Extension Interim Study Results" → SKIP
   "Positive 52-Week Safety Data from License Partner" → SKIP
   "Long-Term Safety and Tolerability Data" → SKIP
   "Reports Successful Results in Vitro" → SKIP (preclinical)
   "In Vivo Results Demonstrate Efficacy" → SKIP (animal studies)
   "Small Molecules Show Potential for Disease" → SKIP (speculative)
   "Strengthens Clinical Development Pathway Following Positive Phase 2 Outcomes" → SKIP (no data, vague)
   "Announces Positive Phase 2 Results" → SKIP (no numbers — what does "positive" mean?)
   "Phase 2 Data Shows Encouraging Trends" → SKIP (no specifics)

LINE EXTENSIONS / LABEL EXPANSIONS (Incremental)
├─ Drug already approved, testing for new body parts or populations
├─ "sNDA", "Label expansion", "Additional indication", "Expand Label" for EXISTING drug
├─ "Initiate trial seeking to expand label" = label expansion (NOT new drug)
├─ Even "Phase 3" doesn't matter if it's for label expansion of existing drug
├─ EXCEPTION: New indication in MUCH larger market (rare disease → mass market)
└─ Examples:
   "Phase 3 Results for Drug on Extremities, Neck, and Trunk" → SKIP
   "To Initiate Phase 3 Trial Seeking to Expand Label to Include [Indication]" → SKIP
   "sNDA Submitted for Additional Indication" → SKIP

MEDIA/PUBLICITY
├─ TV features, news program mentions, awareness campaigns
└─ "Featured on", "Highlighted by" → SKIP

===============================================================================
UNIVERSAL SKIP SIGNALS (Bad language patterns)
===============================================================================

NON-COMMITTAL LANGUAGE (100% losers in backtesting)
├─ "Non-binding" = not a real deal
├─ "Enters Into" without specifics = corporate speak
├─ "Memorandum of Understanding" without $ = preliminary
├─ "Framework" / "Collaboration Framework" = NOT a definitive agreement
├─ "Anticipates" / "Potential Royalties" / "Potential Milestones" = speculative projections
├─ "upon execution of a Definitive Agreement" = the real deal DOES NOT EXIST YET
├─ KEY: Dollar amounts tied to "potential" or "anticipated" milestones are WORTHLESS
│   until a definitive agreement is signed. Micro-cap biotechs use inflated milestone
│   numbers to generate press releases. The actual deal often never materialises.
└─ Examples:
   "Signs Non-binding Letter of Intent" → SKIP
   "Signs Collaboration Framework Anticipating $60M in Potential Royalties upon Definitive Agreement" → SKIP (framework, not deal; "anticipates" = speculative; definitive agreement doesn't exist)
   "Signs Exclusive Framework with [Unknown Pharma] for $X Million in Milestones" → SKIP (framework ≠ contract, unknown partner, milestone $ = contingent)

LETTER OF INTENT — NUANCED (50+ winners in backtest)
├─ TRADE: LOI + Acquisition + Named Target (specific deal = credible)
├─ SKIP: LOI + Vague partnership (no commitment)
└─ Examples:
   "LOI To Acquire Majority Stake in ABC Corp" → TRADE (specific)
   "Signs Letter of Intent With Major Partner" → SKIP (no target)


===============================================================================
POSITION SIZING
===============================================================================

Output: TRADE SMALL, TRADE MODERATE, TRADE LARGE, TRADE MAX, or SKIP

SIZE FACTORS:

1. CATALYST TYPE (Most important for biotech)
   - MAX: FDA approval, M&A (being acquired), Phase 3 met primary endpoint
   - LARGE: Fast Track/Breakthrough designation, major pharma partnership,
     Phase 2/3 positive with numbers, government contract (large vs mkt cap),
     Priority Review Voucher sale
   - MODERATE: Phase 2 positive in hot area, hot modality Phase 1 with numbers,
     orphan drug designation, share repurchase
   - SMALL: Hot modality Phase 1 positive (no numbers), analyst upgrade, patent

2. MODALITY MULTIPLIER
   - If hot modality (Tier 1/2): upgrade size by one level
   - Example: Phase 2 positive normally = MODERATE, but ADC Phase 2 = LARGE

3. HEADLINE CONCRETENESS
   - Specific numbers, named counterparties, definitive terms → upgrade
   - Vague "positive results" without specifics → downgrade

4. DOLLAR AMOUNT vs MARKET CAP (When applicable)
   - MAX: Deal value > 50% of market cap
   - LARGE: 25-50%
   - MODERATE: 10-25%
   - SMALL: < 10%

===============================================================================
DECISION RULES
===============================================================================

THINK MODALITY-FIRST, THEN PHASE:
1. Is this a HOT MODALITY? (ADC, GLP-1, radiopharmaceutical, CAR-T autoimmune, etc.)
   → If yes: positive Phase 1+ data is tradeable
   → If no: require Phase 2b+ with specific numbers
2. Is this a HOT THERAPEUTIC AREA? (major cancer, obesity, Alzheimer's, rare disease)
   → If yes: lower the phase threshold for trading
3. Are results POSITIVE with any specificity?
   → "Met endpoint", numbers, "complete response" = TRADE
   → Vague "positive", "encouraging" alone = needs hot modality to trade

ALWAYS TRADE:
- FDA drug approval
- M&A (being acquired)
- Phase 3 met primary endpoint
- Fast Track / Breakthrough Therapy designation
- Major pharma partnership with named company
- Share repurchase/buyback programs = always TRADE (never defensive)

ALWAYS SKIP:
- Earnings / financial results (except extraordinary: >60% growth, swing to profit — TRADE SMALL)
- Offerings / dilution (ordinary PIPEs, RDOs, ATMs)
- IND clearance (permission to start trials, not approval)
- Conferences (future presentations)
- In vitro / in vivo (preclinical)
- Trial initiation with no results ("Initiates", "begins", "doses first patient", "enrolls first patient")
- Extension studies (continuation, not new data)
- "Potential" / "may" / "could" (speculative language)

CONDITIONAL SKIP:
- Safety/tolerability only → SKIP when stand-alone on a generic modality, TRADE when
  the phase is 1/2 in a HOT modality or HOT area (safety IS the primary endpoint at
  early phase for these; see CLINICAL DATA section)

RED FLAGS (weaken any headline):
- "Extension" = not new data
- "Interim" without numbers = preliminary
- "Safety/tolerability" alone = not efficacy
- "License partner" = indirect
- "Open-label" = weaker evidence
- "In vitro" / "in vivo" = ALWAYS SKIP (preclinical)

MATERIAL DEAL OVERRIDE: If the headline describes a NEW deal/order/contract/agreement with a SPECIFIC dollar amount
that is >25% of the company'''s market cap (provided in CONTEXT), it is almost certainly TRADE regardless of whether
the deal type matches the industry-specific trade signals above. Dollar amounts relative to company size are the
strongest signal. Evaluate on merit — do not let unfamiliar deal types cause a SKIP when the numbers are transformational.

RESPOND: TRADE MAX, TRADE LARGE, TRADE MODERATE, TRADE SMALL, or SKIP (no explanation)

├─ Phase 1/2/3 topline results (actual data, not trial initiation)
├─ Pharmacokinetic study results
├─ Bioequivalence study positive
├─ NOTE: "Initiate" / "to begin" / "commence" trial = SKIP (no results yet)

│   Headlines that say "Positive Phase 2 Outcomes" or "Positive Results" without
│   ANY specific numbers (no response rate, no %, no p-value, no endpoint) = SKIP.
│   Winners always have concrete data. "Positive" without numbers is a press release.
│   ✗ "Positive Phase 2 Outcomes" → SKIP (what was positive? No data given)

│   ✓ "86% Clinical Benefit Rate" → TRADE (specific number)

├─ NOTE: INTERIM / PRELIMINARY = SKIP (unless with specific numbers)

│   ✗ "Positive Interim Results from Phase 2 Study" → SKIP (interim, no numbers)
│   ✗ "Reports Positive Preliminary Data" → SKIP (preliminary, no data)
│   ✓ "Interim Analysis Shows 45% Response Rate" → TRADE (specific number)
│   ✓ "Phase 3 Interim: Primary Endpoint Met With p<0.001" → TRADE (pivotal + specific)
└─ Examples:
   "Announces Topline Results from Phase 2 Clinical Trial" → TRADE
   "Completed Pharmacokinetic Study Shows Positive Results" → TRADE
   "To Initiate Phase 3 Clinical Trial" → SKIP (no results, just starting)
   "Phase 3 Trial Seeking to Expand Label" → SKIP (label expansion)
   "Strengthens Clinical Development Pathway Following Positive Phase 2 Outcomes" → SKIP (no data)
   "Announces Positive Phase 2 Results" → SKIP (no numbers)
   "Announces Positive Interim Results from Study" → SKIP (interim, no numbers)

STRATEGIC ALTERNATIVES / ACQUISITION TARGET (Very strong — company is being pursued)
- "Evaluating strategic alternatives" with investment bank engaged = serious M&A process
- "Unsolicited expressions of interest" = buyers are approaching the company
- "Received unsolicited offer/proposal" = concrete acquisition attempt
- "Retained [investment bank] to explore strategic alternatives" = formal sale process
- These signal the company is an ACQUISITION TARGET — one of the strongest catalysts
- Named investment bank (Roth Capital, Goldman, Lazard, etc.) = credible, not just talk
- NUANCE: "Evaluating strategic alternatives" ALONE without bank/advisor = weaker (TRADE SMALL)
- NUANCE: "Exploring strategic options" in an earnings call = often defensive language = SKIP
- Examples:
   "Engages Investment Bank To Review Unsolicited Interest And Evaluate Strategic Alternatives" -> TRADE LARGE
   "Retains Roth Capital To Explore Strategic Alternatives" -> TRADE MODERATE
   "Received Unsolicited Acquisition Proposal" -> TRADE LARGE
   "Board Evaluating Strategic Alternatives" (no bank named) -> TRADE SMALL
   "Exploring Strategic Options" (in earnings context) -> SKIP (defensive)

SHARE REPURCHASE (Bullish signal — ALWAYS TRADE, no exceptions except earnings)

├─ Reduces float, increases EPS = bullish
├─ "Reactivates" / "Resumes" / "Reinstates" share repurchase = TRADE (paused program restarting = bullish)
├─ NOTE: "Capital Allocation Strategy" with share repurchase = TRADE (not defensive)

ENTITY-RICH PARTNERSHIPS (Signal Density Override)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Some headlines lack a literal partner name but compensate with MULTIPLE
concrete signals. When 4+ of these entity signals are present, TRADE even
if the partner is described by tier rather than by name.

ENTITY SIGNALS (count how many are present):
├─ (1) Definitive agreement language ("commercial agreement", "licensing
│      agreement", "signed", "definitive" — NOT "framework", "potential")
├─ (2) Tiered or named major partner ("Top 5/10/20 Pharmaceutical",
│      "Fortune 500", "Major Global" — NDA-restricted naming, not vagueness)
├─ (3) AI/ML as core function ("AI drug discovery", "AI-powered",
│      "machine learning", "identify therapeutic targets")
├─ (4) Named major disease with massive TAM (depression, cancer, diabetes,
│      obesity, Alzheimer's, heart disease, autoimmune, Parkinson's, ALS
│      — household-name conditions the general public knows and fears)
├─ (5) Specific deliverable or function ("identify and prioritize
│      therapeutic targets", "develop companion diagnostic", etc.)
└─ (6) Dollar amount stated

SCORING:
├─ 4+ signals → TRADE MODERATE
├─ 5+ signals → TRADE LARGE
└─ 3 signals with AI/ML core + tiered major partner → TRADE SMALL

WHY: "Top 5 Global Pharmaceutical Company" is NDA-restricted naming, not
vagueness. Companies working with Pfizer/Novartis/Roche often cannot name
the partner pre-announcement. The tier descriptor + definitive agreement
language + specific disease + specific deliverable = compound conviction
that overrides the missing literal name.

STILL SKIP even with high entity count:
├─ "Framework" / "Potential" / "Anticipates" = non-committal, invalidates all
├─ No disease AND no deliverable AND no $ (AI + partner tier alone = too thin)
└─ "Explores" / "Evaluates" / "In discussions" = no commitment

Examples:
   "Commercial Agreement with Top 5 Global Pharmaceutical Company to Identify
    and Prioritize Therapeutic Target in Major Depressive Disorder"
    → TRADE LARGE (5 signals: agreement ✓, tiered partner ✓, AI function ✓,
      major disease ✓, specific deliverable ✓)
   "Agreement with Top 10 Pharma to Deploy AI for Oncology Drug Discovery"
    → TRADE MODERATE (4 signals: agreement, tiered partner, AI, major disease)
   "Framework with Top 5 Pharmaceutical to Explore AI Applications"
    → SKIP ("Framework" + "Explore" = non-committal, invalidates all)
   "AI Partnership with Unknown Biotech for Data Analysis"
    → SKIP (no partner tier, no disease, no specific deliverable)

SKIP SIGNALS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

OFFERINGS/FINANCING → SKIP ALL
├─ Offerings are DILUTIVE - they create new shares and dilute existing holders
├─ Even "at market" offerings signal the company needs cash (weakness)
├─ "Registered Direct Offering" = institutional dump, always bearish
├─ SKIP: Prices offering, Closes placement, PIPE, private placement
└─ Examples:
   "Prices Public Offering" → SKIP (dilution)
   "Announces Registered Direct Offering" → SKIP (dilution)

EARNINGS (SKIP unless record results with specific $ amounts/percentages)
├─ EPS beats/misses, Revenue beats/misses
├─ Quarterly results, Raised/lowered guidance
├─ Plain earnings → SKIP (record results with specific $ amounts/percentages = TRADE SMALL)
│   EXCEPTION: If the headline contains SPECIFIC numbers showing EXCEPTIONAL
│   performance (>100% revenue/sales growth, profit swing from loss to profit,
│   or similarly extraordinary concrete metrics), TRADE SMALL. The numbers must
│   be IN the headline itself — not vague "beats estimates" but actual figures
│   like "171% Revenue Growth" or "Sales Surged 146%".
│   ✓ "171% YoY Revenue Growth, $2.7M Net Profit" → TRADE SMALL (extraordinary)
│   ✓ "Unit Sales Surged 146% Year-Over-Year" → TRADE SMALL (extraordinary)
│   ✗ "Q4 EPS Beats Estimate" → SKIP (no extraordinary numbers)
│   ✗ "Reports Record Revenue" → SKIP (vague, no specific growth rate)
│   ✗ "Revenue Up 15% YoY" → SKIP (solid but not extraordinary)
└─ Examples:
   "Q4 EPS Beats Estimate" → SKIP
   "Raises Revenue Guidance" → SKIP

CONFERENCES
├─ "To present at", J.P. Morgan Conference
├─ Any future presentation
└─ Examples:
   "To Present at the 44th Annual J.P. Morgan Healthcare Conference" → SKIP

FDA MEETINGS (Procedural - NOT approvals or milestones)
├─ "Type A Meeting", "Type B Meeting", "Type C Meeting" = FDA consultation meetings
├─ "Meeting Scheduled", "Meeting Granted", "Meeting Requested" = procedural only
├─ These are ROUTINE administrative events, NOT regulatory decisions
├─ A meeting is NOT an approval, NOT a breakthrough designation, NOT a milestone
├─ The market does not react to scheduled meetings - only to outcomes
└─ Examples:
   "FDA Type C Meeting Scheduled for Drug Candidate" → SKIP (just a meeting)
   "Company Requests Type B Meeting With FDA" → SKIP (procedural)
   "FDA Grants Type A Meeting" → SKIP (administrative)
   "Pre-IND Meeting Scheduled" → SKIP (early stage, just planning)

ADMINISTRATIVE
├─ Inducement grants, Nasdaq compliance
├─ Board/Executive appointments (unless interim during crisis)
└─ Examples:
   "Reports Inducement Grants Under Nasdaq Listing Rule" → SKIP

LEGAL
├─ Class action, Investigation
├─ Any law firm mentioned
└─ Examples:
   "ALVO Investigation: Investors Encouraged to Contact" → SKIP

ROUTINE AGREEMENTS
├─ Product purchase agreement extensions (not new deals)
└─ Examples:
   "Extends Product Purchase Agreement Through 2026" → SKIP (routine)

UNIVERSAL SKIP SIGNALS (Bad language patterns - applies to all headlines)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

NON-COMMITTAL LANGUAGE (100% losers in backtesting)
├─ "Non-binding" = not a real deal, frequently falls through
├─ "Memorandum of Understanding" without $ = preliminary, no commitment
└─ Examples:
   "Signs Non-binding Letter of Intent" → SKIP
   "Signs MOU for Future Cooperation" → SKIP (vague)

LETTER OF INTENT (LOI) - NUANCED (50+ winners in backtest)
├─ NOT all LOIs are bad - acquisition LOIs with named targets WIN
├─ TRADE: LOI + Acquisition + Named Target (specific deal = credible)
├─ SKIP: LOI + Vague partnership without specifics (no commitment)
├─ Key difference: Acquisition intent with named target shows real deal
└─ Examples:
   "LOI To Acquire Majority Stake in ABC Corp" → TRADE (specific target)
   "Signs LOI For Strategic Acquisition of XYZ" → TRADE (named target)
   "Signs Letter of Intent With Major Partner" → SKIP (no target named)
   "Enters Into LOI For Potential Investment" → SKIP (vague, no specifics)


CASH OUTFLOWS (Spending money, not making it)
├─ "Acquires" / "To Acquire" = company SPENDING money
├─ EXCEPTION: If being acquired (target) → TRADE
└─ Examples:
   "To Acquire Biotech Startup" → SKIP
   "To Be Acquired For $X Per Share" → TRADE



DILUTION/OFFERINGS (Company raising cash = stock goes DOWN)
├─ "Securities purchase agreement" = private placement (PIPE) = dilution
├─ "Prices offering", "Closes offering", "Underwritten offering" = dilution
├─ "Registered direct offering", "At-the-market offering" = dilution
├─ "Private placement", "PIPE", "Gross proceeds", "Net proceeds" = dilution
├─ Even "at market" offerings signal the company needs cash (weakness)
├─ The stock almost ALWAYS drops on offerings - sellers front-run dilution
└─ Examples:
   "Enters Into Securities Purchase Agreement" → SKIP (dilution)
   "Prices $50M Public Offering" → SKIP (dilution)
   "Announces Registered Direct Offering" → SKIP (dilution)
   "Closes Private Placement" → SKIP (dilution)

VAGUE PARTNERSHIPS (No concrete deliverables)
├─ "Partnership for innovation" = vague, no specific product or revenue
├─ "Strategic collaboration" without specifics = corporate fluff
├─ Unknown counterparties with no dollar amounts = not credible
├─ REQUIRE: Named customer/partner + (specific product OR dollar amount)
├─ EXCEPTION — TIERED PARTNERS: "Top 5/10/20 Pharmaceutical", "Top-Tier
│   Pharma", "Major Global Company", "Fortune 500" descriptors are NDA-
│   restricted naming, NOT vagueness. Treat as valid partner signal when
│   combined with definitive agreement language + additional entity signals.
│   See ENTITY-RICH PARTNERSHIPS in TRADE SIGNALS above.
├─ NOTE: A dollar amount is NOT required when the partner is a named major company
│   (Fortune 500, major pharma, major health system, government agency) AND the headline
│   describes a specific product, therapeutic area, or scope. The partner name IS the signal
│   — major partner validation is transformational for small-cap companies even without
│   disclosed dollar terms.
└─ Examples:
   "Partnership With Unknown Company For Innovation" → SKIP (vague)
   "Strategic Collaboration To Explore Opportunities" → SKIP (no specifics)
   "Licenses Technology to Medtronic for Cardiac Monitoring" → TRADE (named major + specific scope, no $ needed)
   "Partners With Mayo Clinic for Clinical Validation Study" → TRADE (named major institution + specific scope)
   "Partners With Fortune 500 For $20M Platform Deal" → TRADE (specific)


KEY RULE: When uncertain, lean TRADE SMALL rather than SKIP.
Position sizing handles conviction — use SMALL for marginal headlines.
Only SKIP headlines that are clearly not catalysts (earnings, offerings, conferences, dilution, non-binding).

===============================================================================
POSITION SIZING (Output: TRADE SMALL, TRADE MODERATE, TRADE LARGE, TRADE MAX, or SKIP)
===============================================================================

You must classify BOTH whether to trade AND the position size.
Position size is based on headline strength and conviction level.

SIZE FACTORS - Consider ALL of the following:

1. HEADLINE CONCRETENESS (Most important)
   - MAX: Specific dollar amounts, named counterparties, definitive terms
   - LARGE: Specific details but missing some elements (e.g., no $ but named partner)
   - MODERATE: Reasonably specific but some vagueness
   - SMALL: Tradeable but lacks specificity

2. DOLLAR AMOUNT RELATIVE TO COMPANY (When applicable)
   You will receive company market cap in the CONTEXT section.
   - MAX: Deal value > 50% of market cap (transformational)
   - LARGE: Deal value 25-50% of market cap (major)
   - MODERATE: Deal value 10-25% of market cap (significant)
   - SMALL: Deal value < 10% of market cap (modest)
   Note: Not all headline types have dollar amounts - that's OK. Use other factors.

3. CATALYST STRENGTH (Industry-specific)
   - MAX: Strongest catalysts for this industry (see TRADE SIGNALS above)
   - LARGE: Strong catalysts
   - MODERATE: Moderate catalysts
   - SMALL: Weaker but still tradeable catalysts

4. COUNTERPARTY QUALITY (When applicable)
   - MAX: Fortune 100, major government (DOD, DOE, NASA), big pharma, big tech
   - LARGE: Fortune 500, major industry players
   - MODERATE: Known companies, mid-tier partners
   - SMALL: Unknown or smaller counterparties

EXAMPLES OF SIZE DETERMINATION:
- "$140M contract with DOD" for $50M market cap company → TRADE MAX (280% of mkt cap, DOD)
- "$20M partnership with Microsoft" for $100M company → TRADE LARGE (20% + big tech)
- "Partners with regional distributor" (no $) → TRADE SMALL (vague, unknown partner)
- "FDA Approval for new drug" → TRADE MAX (strongest biotech catalyst)
- "Wins contract" (no $ amount, no customer name) → TRADE SMALL (vague)
- "Signs $5M deal with Fortune 500" for $200M company → TRADE MODERATE (2.5%, good partner)

CONCRETE > VAGUE:
Headlines with specific numbers, names, and terms are more reliable.
When in doubt about size, ask: "How concrete is this headline?"

strongest signal. Evaluate on merit — do not let unfamiliar deal types cause a SKIP when the numbers are transformational.

Rules:

PRINCIPLE-FIRST RULE — THE NATURE OF THE EVENT MATTERS, NOT THE WORDING (APPLIES TO EVERY TYPE):
For every type below, the nouns, verbs, and phrasings shown in examples are ILLUSTRATIVE, not exhaustive. Your job is to recognize the underlying ECONOMIC PRINCIPLE of the headline, then classify it based on what is happening — not which specific word the company used to describe it. A "program" at a major retailer, a "rollout", a "supply arrangement", a "win", a "selection", and a "deal" are all the SAME economic event as a "contract" when the substance is the same: the company receives new revenue-generating business from a named counterparty. The same principle holds for every other type — product launches, partnerships, approvals, clinical data, licensing, designations, and so on. Do not require the exact nouns or verbs shown in examples. If a headline is PRINCIPLE-EQUIVALENT to an example (same economic nature, different wording), classify it the same type. Trust your understanding of the event. Overfitting to specific words causes you to miss the same catalyst dressed in different language. The listed terms and examples are anchors, not a closed set — synonyms and re-wordings of the same principle always count.

PRECEDENCE RULES — WHEN A HEADLINE FITS MULTIPLE TYPES:
Real headlines often bundle multiple things. Apply these in order:

1. AI/ROBOTICS OVERRIDES PARTNERSHIP: When a headline qualifies BOTH as `partnership` AND as `ai_breakthrough` Path B (named partner + concrete AI/robotics domain + clear growth signal), classify as `ai_breakthrough`. "Partnership" / "collaboration" / "tie-up" is only the legal/structural label; the AI/robotics content is the actual market catalyst. Do not let the word "partnership" or "collaboration" in the headline mask an AI/robotics catalyst underneath.
   Example: "Enters Strategic Collaboration with ICreate Education Technology to Advance AI Robotics Learning in North America" → ai_breakthrough (Path B: named partner + "AI Robotics Learning" domain + "Advance" growth signal). NOT partnership.

2. CONCRETE SPECIFIC CATALYSTS WIN OVER NARRATIVE: When a headline bundles multiple events (expansion + investment + patent grant, or rebrand + launch + earnings, etc.), prioritize the most concrete NAMED specific catalyst. General precedence when multiple types apply to the same headline:
   fda_approval > fda_clearance > fda_designation > clinical_breakthrough > pivotal_trial_data > merger_agreement > acquisition_target > acquisition_with_revenue_generating_business > government_contract > military_contract > major_contract > stock_buyback > patent_granted > ai_breakthrough > ai_rebranding > strategic_investment_received > licensing_agreement > revenue_milestone > strategic_alternatives_review > reimbursement_pathway > peer_reviewed_publication > listing_compliance_regained > partnership > product_launch > other

3. PATENT GRANTS ARE ALWAYS SPECIFIC: If the headline contains "granted patent", "issued patent", "patent grant", or similar final-grant language for a NAMED asset, classify as `patent_granted` regardless of other topics also mentioned. The patent grant is the dominant catalyst — it wins over expansion language, investment language, product narrative.
   Example: "Expands into Fleet Operations with Accelevate Investment and BEN's Newly Granted Patent" → patent_granted (NOT "other" and NOT product_launch). The Newly Granted Patent is the specific catalyst; the expansion and investment are narrative framing around it.

UNIVERSAL RULE — NEW ANNOUNCEMENTS ONLY (APPLIES TO EVERY TYPE):
We ONLY trade NEW, FORWARD-LOOKING events the market has not yet priced in. If a headline REPORTS on something that ALREADY HAPPENED, it is NOT tradeable — classify as "other" regardless of how bullish the event sounds. The market prices in events at announcement time; backward-looking reports are not catalysts.
  PAST-TENSE RED FLAGS: "achieved", "completed", "has repurchased", "returned $X to shareholders", "bought back", "previously", "last quarter", "during Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4", "year-to-date results", "delivered on", "fulfilled". These describe what already happened — NOT a new event.
  FORWARD-LOOKING GREEN FLAGS: "announces", "launches", "commences", "authorizes", "awarded", "wins", "enters into", "signs", "initiates", "receives approval", "grants". These describe something happening NOW.
  Ask: "Is this headline telling the market something NEW it didn't know, or REPORTING on something that already happened?" If reporting → "other".

FUND/TRUST RULE (APPLIES TO EVERY TYPE):
If any party in the headline is a closed-end fund, investment fund, ETF, or trust (names typically containing "Fund", "Trust", "Income Fund", "Capital Trust", "Strategy Fund", "Investment Fund"), the event is routine asset management housekeeping — NOT a tradeable catalyst. This covers fund mergers/consolidations, conditional tender offers by funds, preferred-share tender offers, and NAV discount management. Classify as "other". Only OPERATING COMPANIES (those selling products or services) generate tradeable mergers, buybacks, or contracts.

CASH FLOW DIRECTION (APPLIES TO EVERY TYPE):
We trade events where the company RECEIVES value. Events where the company SPENDS money (acquisitions, outward investments, facility purchases) are cash outflows — classify as acquisition_announced, offering_announced, or "other", never a tradeable contract type. EXCEPTION: stock_buyback is cash outflow but bullish (company buying its own shares reduces float, signals confidence).

PRIORITY RULE — MERGER vs ACQUISITION vs ACQUISITION TARGET:
Three distinct events; classify correctly:
- merger_agreement: Two named OPERATING COMPANIES combining. Language: "merger agreement", "agree to merge", "merger of equals", "all-stock merger", "sign definitive merger agreement". Neither spends cash. MUST be the INITIAL ANNOUNCEMENT — completions ("closes merger", "merger finalized"), terminations ("terminates merger", "called off"), amendments ("extends merger deadline", "revises terms"), regulatory challenges ("FTC challenges", "DOJ sues to block"), and status updates on existing mergers all → "other".
- acquisition_announced: Company named FIRST is DOING the acquiring (cash outflow, bearish for acquirer). Language: "acquires", "to acquire", "closes acquisition of", "signs LOI to acquire", "secures ownership of", "completes purchase of", "re-activates acquisition". CRITICAL: If ANY acquisition language appears, classify as acquisition_announced, acquisition_target, or acquisition_with_revenue_generating_business — NEVER major_contract / government_contract / defense_order, regardless of revenue targets, dollar amounts, or defense context. If the acquisition brings a concrete, specific inbound revenue stream (dated forward revenue, ARR, or contracted revenue explicitly tied to the target or combined entity) AND that stream is material enough to reprice the company, use acquisition_with_revenue_generating_business (see definition below). Default to acquisition_announced for all other acquirer-side deals.
- acquisition_target: Company named FIRST is BEING acquired (bullish for target). Language: "to be acquired", "agrees to be acquired", "tender offer for X", "receives buyout offer". If headline says "Y and X announce acquisition" and Y is buying X, classify X as acquisition_target.
Examples: "SiriusXM and Liberty Media Sign Definitive Merger Agreement" → merger_agreement. "Sprint and T-Mobile Announce Merger of Equals" → merger_agreement. "Completes Previously Announced Merger" → other. "Terminates Merger Agreement with ABC" → other.

PRIORITY RULE — BUYBACK vs OFFERING (OPPOSITE EVENTS):
Same word "offer" can mean opposite things — check DIRECTION of cash and shares:
- stock_buyback: Company BUYING BACK its own common stock (bullish — reduces float). Language: "Self Tender Offer To Purchase Shares", "Commences Tender Offer To Purchase...", "Share Repurchase Program", "Board Authorizes Stock Buyback", "Dutch Auction Tender Offer". MUST be a NEW, FORWARD-LOOKING authorization (past-tense buyback reports → "other" per UNIVERSAL RULE). Operating companies and COMMON stock only — fund tender offers and preferred-share tender offers → "other" per FUND/TRUST RULE.
- offering_announced: Company ISSUING new shares (dilutive, bearish). Language: "Prices Public Offering", "Registered Direct Offering", "Prices Secondary Offering", "Closes Private Placement", "Securities Purchase Agreement".
CRITICAL DIRECTION CHECK: "Tender Offer To PURCHASE" → stock_buyback (company buying). "Offering OF shares" / "Prices Offering" → offering_announced (company selling). NEVER classify share repurchase/buyback as offering_announced.
Examples that ARE stock_buyback: "Apple Announces $100B Share Repurchase Program", "Board Authorizes $50M Stock Buyback", "Tesla Commences Tender Offer To Purchase Up To 10M Shares".

- government_contract: Company has been NEWLY AWARDED or WON a concrete contract from a US federal/state agency, NASA, DARPA, DoD, or well-known international agency (NATO, UN, major allied nations). Must be a NEW deal the market has not seen before, with the company RECEIVING revenue for delivering products or services. Look for award language: "awarded", "wins", "receives contract", "selected for contract", "secures contract" — but recognize that any principle-equivalent wording also qualifies (see PRINCIPLE-FIRST RULE). Apply the CONTRACT GUARDRAILS (below) before confirming — if it fails ANY of (a)-(n), classify as "other" instead.
- military_contract: Company has been NEWLY AWARDED a contract from a military branch (Army, Navy, Air Force, Space Force, Marines) or for military products (weapons, drones, munitions, counter-UAS, satellites for defense, foreign military sales). Apply CONTRACT GUARDRAILS (below) — must be a NEW award.
- defense_order: Company has received a NEW order from a defense contractor or for defense products (not government directly). Apply CONTRACT GUARDRAILS (below).
- major_contract: A NEW commercial arrangement where the subject company will RECEIVE revenue from a counterparty. The SUBSTANCE is what matters: any arrangement whose economic nature is "company X will now earn money from counterparty Y for products/services" qualifies, regardless of whether the headline calls it a contract, program, order, agreement, rollout, launch at [retailer], supply deal, win, selection, designation as supplier, or any other synonym. The nouns and verbs listed here are common examples, not a closed set — any phrasing describing the same underlying event qualifies. Do NOT require the specific word "contract" — a "program at Target" is the same principle as a "contract with Target".

  MATERIALITY — at least ONE of the following must be present:
    (1) SPECIFIC DOLLAR AMOUNT — even a modest $ figure signals the deal is concrete enough to quantify.
    (2) NAMED MAJOR COUNTERPARTY — use your own judgment of the counterparty's scale and recognition. Household-name companies (large national/global retailers, tech giants, major pharma, top banks, QSR chains, major media, major industrial firms) carry automatic materiality on their name alone — no $ amount needed. Companies that come to mind as household-name anchors include Target, Walmart, Amazon, Costco, Home Depot, Microsoft, Apple, Google, Pfizer, Moderna, McDonald's, Starbucks, JPMorgan — but this is NOT a closed list. Use your pretraining knowledge: any company of comparable recognition and scale qualifies, and you should actively infer beyond the listed anchors. If you recognize the counterparty as a major institution most readers would know, it qualifies. Smaller or unknown counterparties do NOT satisfy this criterion alone — either a $ amount or a "major"/"significant"/"landmark" qualifier (see (4)) must also be present.
    (3) GOVERNMENT/MILITARY ORDER — government entities qualify regardless of dollar amount (use government_contract / military_contract / defense_order instead of major_contract).
    (4) MATERIALITY QUALIFIER — the headline describes the deal as "major", "significant", "landmark", "transformational", "flagship", or equivalent. Self-declared materiality from the subject company is a valid signal because companies deliberately use these words when the deal IS material to them. The qualifier carries weight because the company chose to apply it.

  Any ONE of (1)–(4) is sufficient. A deal with a household-name counterparty is ALWAYS major_contract — no dollar amount needed. A deal self-described as "major" is ALWAYS major_contract — no dollar amount needed. The classic case of a small-cap supplier winning shelf space, supply rights, or a program at a tier-one retailer is ALWAYS major_contract, even with no $ figure.

  EXCLUDE: contract renewals/extensions, PRV sales, pure royalty-only arrangements (ongoing % with no upfront $), M&A, FDA pathway agreements, vague "strategic" partnerships without $ amounts or named major counterparty, acquisitions (company SPENDING money), vague deployments with unnamed customers, and ALL financing/lending arrangements. CRITICAL DISTINCTION: non-dilutive INCOME (contracted revenue the company will EARN) IS major_contract. Non-dilutive CAPITAL (loans, credit facilities, term loans, revolving credit, debt financing, capital from financial firms like Blue Owl, Apollo, Ares, Blackstone) is NOT major_contract — classify as debt_restructuring. If the headline mentions "acquisition of" or "acquires" ANYWHERE, it is NEVER major_contract — use acquisition_announced or acquisition_target.

  Examples that ARE government_contract / military_contract / major_contract (note the variety of wording — the principle is the same):
    "Awarded $50M U.S. Army Contract for Drone Systems" → military_contract
    "Wins $15M NASA Contract for Satellite Components" → government_contract
    "Wins Major New Fresh-Cut Herb Program at Target Stores" → major_contract (household-name counterparty + 'major' qualifier, no $ needed)
    "Selected as Exclusive Supplier for Walmart Private Label Cosmetics" → major_contract (household-name counterparty + exclusivity)
    "Signs $20M Deal With Microsoft" → major_contract ($ + named tier-one)
    "Chosen by McDonald's to Supply Regional Packaging" → major_contract (household-name, no $ needed)
    "Adds Over $2.6 Million in Contracted, Non-Dilutive Income Through Sublease Agreement" → major_contract ($ + contracted income, NOT financing)
  Examples that are NOT (classify as "other" or the noted type):
    "Advances U.S. Homeland Security Contract with Shipment of First PowerBuoy" → other (delivery milestone, guardrail (a))
    "Extends $10M Army Maintenance Contract" → other (extension, guardrail (d))
    "Submits Bid for $100M Navy Program" → other (bid, guardrail (c))
    "Receives $1.9 Million First Purchase Order from Luminia" → other (unknown counterparty, guardrail (l))
    "Secures $500M in Non-Dilutive Capital from Blue Owl" → debt_restructuring (borrowing, not earning)
    "Re-Activates 70% Acquisition of Tekne, Advancing Defense Platform Targeting €100M+ Revenue" → acquisition_announced

CONTRACT GUARDRAILS — APPLY AFTER IDENTIFYING A POTENTIAL CONTRACT:
If a headline would otherwise qualify for government_contract / military_contract / defense_order / major_contract, check these guardrails before confirming. If ANY apply, classify as "other" (or noted alternative). These are safety rails after pattern recognition, not leading skepticism.
  (a) EXISTING-DEAL MILESTONES: Shipping, delivering, deploying, installing, advancing, reaffirming, or progress reports on an existing contract/partnership/program/joint venture. The deal was priced in at announcement — operational milestones are not new catalysts. ("Ships First Unit Under...", "Advances Contract with Shipment of...", "Enters Deployment Phase", "Reaffirms $50M Contract", "Provides Update on Program", "On Track to Deliver Under Contract")
  (b) PRELIMINARY/NON-BINDING: Not yet finalized. ("LOI", "MOU", "Framework Agreement", "In Discussions With", "In Negotiations With", "Exploring Opportunity", "Pathway to", "Potential $X Contract", "Non-binding")
  (c) BIDS/PROPOSALS: Submissions not yet awarded. ("Submits Bid for...", "Responds to RFP", "Shortlisted for...", "Selected to Compete for...")
  (d) EXTENSIONS/RENEWALS/MODIFICATIONS: Not new business. ("Extends Contract", "Renews Agreement", "Receives Modification to Existing Contract", "Follow-on Order")
  (e) PR/MARKETING MENTIONING A COUNTERPARTY: Company talking about relevance without an actual award. ("Highlights Relevance to DoD", "Positioned to Benefit from Government Spending", "Showcases Technology for Military", "Presents at Government Conference", "Strategic Relevance Amid Tensions")
  (f) PERSONNEL/FACILITY: Hiring ("Hires Former DoD Official", "Appoints Military Advisor") or facility openings ("Opens Facility to Support DoD") — not contract awards.
  (g) ACCOUNTING/BACKLOG: Revenue recognition or pipeline metrics from existing contracts. ("Records Revenue from Contract", "Government Revenue Up 20%", "Backlog Reaches $500M", "Order Book Totals $200M") — accounting, not new catalysts.
  (h) FOREIGN DEVELOPING-NATION DEALS without a specific dollar amount (Liberia, Nigeria, small island nations, etc.) → "other".
  (i) EXPLORATION/MINING/RESOURCE RIGHTS: Licenses, blocks, concessions — company SPENDING to explore, not receiving revenue.
  (j) CERTIFICATIONS/DESIGNATIONS alone without a contract award ("Added to Approved Vendor List") → "other" at triage level (sector prompt may still trade these).
  (k) REVENUE PROJECTIONS FROM EXISTING BUSINESS: Speculative forecasts, not concrete contract values. ("Represents up to $80M revenue", "Expected to generate $X", "Combined ventures represent $X", "Targeting $X in revenue"). "Up to" / "represents" are red flags. A new contract has a DEFINED VALUE agreed at signing.
  (l) UNRECOGNIZED COUNTERPARTY PURCHASE ORDERS: A purchase order from a non-government, non-household-name counterparty needs BOTH a $ amount AND a materiality qualifier, OR the counterparty must be a recognized major institution.

TIEBREAKER — If unsure between military_contract and government_contract, prefer military_contract for anything involving armed forces, weapons, or defense systems.
- acquisition_target: Company named FIRST is BEING acquired. See PRIORITY RULE — MERGER vs ACQUISITION above for language and examples.
- acquisition_announced: Company named FIRST is DOING the acquiring (cash outflow, bearish). See PRIORITY RULE — MERGER vs ACQUISITION above. When the headline mentions vague revenue targets, soft forward language ("targeting", "potential", "up to"), or narrative growth framing without a firm numeric anchor, classify as acquisition_announced. Only when the inbound revenue is explicit, specific, and dated/ARR/contracted does the exception at acquisition_with_revenue_generating_business apply.
- acquisition_with_revenue_generating_business: EXCEPTION to the "acquirer = cash outflow = bearish" rule. Company named FIRST is DOING the acquiring, AND the acquired business brings a CONCRETE, SPECIFIC, FIRM inbound revenue stream that is material enough that the market reprices the acquirer around the combined entity. The substance: even though cash flows out, a materially larger revenue stream flows in, and the market treats this as a growth catalyst rather than a cash drain.
  REQUIRES ALL of:
    (1) Clear acquisition language by the subject company (acquires, announces acquisition of, closes acquisition of, etc.) — same triggers as acquisition_announced.
    (2) A SPECIFIC NUMERIC REVENUE FIGURE tied to the target or combined entity, with a FIRM anchor — one of:
        - Dated forward revenue guidance ("$280M Revenue by 2027", "Targets $X Revenue in FY27")
        - ARR / annualized run rate ("Adding $100M ARR", "Annual Revenue Run Rate of $50M")
        - Contracted / recognized revenue ("Adding $75M Contracted Revenue", "Recurring Revenue of $40M")
    (3) The revenue figure is the subject of the headline, not a throwaway phrase. Vague framings ("high-growth business", "revenue-generating operations", "valuable pipeline") do NOT qualify.
  SOFT / VAGUE REVENUE LANGUAGE (keeps it as acquisition_announced, NOT this type):
    "targeting $X", "up to $X", "represents $X", "potential $X revenue", "expected to generate", "combined ventures represent" — all fail requirement (2). A firm anchor is a defined value the acquirer is willing to put its name behind; "targeting" is aspirational.
  NOTE ON MARKET-CAP MATERIALITY: Triage does not know the subject company's market cap. The sector prompt (which receives market cap in its context) enforces the materiality check — typically the inbound revenue must be at least ~50% of current market cap to justify overriding the default acquisition-is-bearish behavior. Triage's job is only to flag the headline as a candidate based on language.
  Examples that ARE acquisition_with_revenue_generating_business:
    "Announces US$1.5 Billion AI Acquisition, Adding High Growth Business and Targets $280M Revenue by 2027" — dated forward revenue anchor ($280M by 2027)
    "Acquires X Corp, Adding $100M Annualized Revenue Run Rate" — ARR anchor
    "Closes Acquisition of Y, Adding $50M in Contracted Recurring Revenue" — contracted revenue anchor
  Examples that are NOT (classify as acquisition_announced):
    "Acquires Tekne, Advancing Defense Platform Targeting €100M+ Revenue" — "Targeting €100M+" is soft/aspirational, fails requirement (2)
    "Announces Acquisition of ABC to Drive Future Growth" — no revenue figure, fails (2)
    "Acquires Startup, Expected to Generate Material Revenue" — vague, fails (2)
    "Completes Acquisition of High-Margin Business" — no figure, fails (2)
- product_launch: Company brings a NEW product, service, platform, or business division to market for the first time. The SUBSTANCE is what matters: any verb describing the act of bringing something new into existence qualifies — "launches", "introduces", "deploys", "unveils", "debuts", "rolls out", "releases", "announces availability of", "goes live with", or any principle-equivalent wording. Do NOT require a specific verb — if the economic event is "this product/service did not exist commercially before and now it does", it qualifies. REQUIRES: a concrete product/service description AND at least ONE of: (1) specific revenue or transaction figures ("$2M in first two months", "$500K monthly recurring"), (2) named customers or user counts ("500 active merchants", "partnership with [named company]"), (3) specific product capability or function described (not just "new platform" — what does it DO?). The product must be NEW — not an update to an existing product. This includes tech-enhanced divisions (e.g., "AI-enabled MICE Division with $2M revenue") where the launch + revenue are the catalyst, not the AI component itself.
  IMPORTANT: If the product being launched IS an AI/robotics/ML system and meets ai_breakthrough Path A criteria, classify as ai_breakthrough instead — the AI technology is the core catalyst, not just the launch event.
  Examples that ARE product_launch: "Launches AI-Enabled MICE Division, Achieves Over $2M in First Two Month Transaction Values" (concrete revenue + specific division — AI is secondary to the revenue), "Deploys New SaaS Platform With 200 Enterprise Customers" (named traction), "Introduces Autonomous Delivery Service, Completes 10,000 Deliveries" (concrete results)
  Examples that are NOT product_launch (classify as "other"): "Announces Plans to Launch New Product" (future, not launched), "Updates Platform With New Features" (update, not new product), "Introduces New Service" (no specifics, no revenue, no traction)
  Examples that are ai_breakthrough NOT product_launch: "Launch of Vinci AI Vision System with Delivery of First Humanoid Robot to Ericsson" (AI/robotics IS the product — classify as ai_breakthrough)
- ai_breakthrough: Headlines where AI/robotics/machine-learning is the CORE catalyst — not a buzzword. Must describe a NEW event the market has not seen before. Two paths qualify:
  PATH A — NEW PRODUCT LAUNCH: Company NEWLY launches, deploys, or unveils a specific AI product, tool, agent, or platform for the FIRST TIME. REQUIRES at least TWO of: (1) named AI product/tool/agent, (2) specific function it performs, (3) concrete results claim. The product must be NEW — not an update, milestone, or performance report on an existing AI product.
  PATH B — NEW AI PARTNERSHIP: Company NEWLY announces a collaboration, partnership, or expansion that is SPECIFICALLY about building or deploying AI/robotics/ML capabilities. REQUIRES ALL of: (1) a named partner or counterparty, (2) a concrete AI/robotics domain (e.g. "AI tutoring", "robotics education", "ML drug discovery"), (3) clear growth signal (expand, launch, deploy, build, develop, roll out — NOT just "explore" or "evaluate"). The partnership must be NEWLY ANNOUNCED — not an update on or expansion of an existing AI partnership.
  EXCLUSION — NOT ai_breakthrough (always classify as "other"):
    (1) MILESTONES/UPDATES/EXTENSIONS ON EXISTING AI: Any performance metrics, feature updates, version releases, user counts, expansions to new markets, or renewals of AI products/partnerships that were already launched. The market priced in the product at launch time. ("AI Platform Processes 1M Transactions", "Updates AI Agent with New Features", "Expands AI Deal with Microsoft to Europe", "Renews AI Alliance with NVIDIA", "Releases Version 2.0 of AI Tool")
    (2) PRELIMINARY/NON-BINDING AI: MOUs, pilot programs, evaluations, exploratory agreements. ("Signs MOU for AI Development", "Exploring AI Integration", "Signs LOI for AI Partnership")
    (3) AI FINANCIAL RESULTS: AI business unit revenue/earnings/growth metrics. ("AI Division Reports Record Revenue", "AI Segment Revenue Up 50%", "AI-Driven Revenue Growth")
  Examples that ARE ai_breakthrough: "Launches AI Agent Revolutionizing Biospecimen Matching" (Path A — NEW launch), "Deploys AI Drug Discovery Platform Achieving 95% Accuracy" (Path A — NEW deployment), "Launch of Vinci AI Vision System with Delivery of First Humanoid Robot to Ericsson" (Path A — NEW AI product + robotics function + delivery to named customer), "Announces Collaboration With Walimaker to Expand AI and Robotics Learning Programs" (Path B — NEW partnership + named partner + concrete AI domain), "Partners With NVIDIA to Build AI-Powered Medical Imaging Pipeline" (Path B — NEW), "Begins Collaboration With Tencent to Support AI Tutor Platform With Real-Time Voice" (Path B — NEW)
  Examples that are NOT ai_breakthrough (classify as "other" or "partnership"): "Announces AI-Powered Solutions" (no specifics), "Integrating AI Into Platform" (vague), "AI-Driven Revenue Growth" (financial results), "Partners With AI Company" (no domain specifics, unnamed capabilities), "Hiring Chief AI Officer" (staffing), "Exploring AI Opportunities" (preliminary), "Leveraging AI To Enhance Operations" (buzzword), "AI Platform Achieves 2M Users" (milestone on existing product), "Expands AI Deal with Microsoft" (existing partnership expansion), "Signs MOU with Samsung for AI Development" (non-binding)
- ai_rebranding: Company formally transitions its corporate identity to signal a strategic pivot into AI, technology, or a high-growth sector. The SUBSTANCE is what matters: any principle-equivalent event where the company takes on a NEW corporate name or identity tied to AI/tech qualifies — regardless of whether the headline uses "rebrands", "renames", "changes name to", "now operating as", "adopts new corporate identity", "unveils new corporate name", "transitions to", "becomes [new name]", or any synonym describing an identity shift. The market treats these as transformational regardless of underlying fundamentals. The headline must describe a COMPLETED identity transition — not a plan, not a product launch, not "exploring AI."
  REQUIRES: (1) a COMPLETED corporate identity change (new name adopted, effective now — not a plan for the future), AND (2) the new identity clearly signals a pivot to AI, technology, blockchain, quantum, or similar high-growth theme.
  MUST NOT be ai_rebranding if: company just adds AI features to existing products (ai_breakthrough or other), mentions AI in a partnership/product context without renaming (use appropriate type), or announces plans to rebrand in the future (other).
  Examples that ARE ai_rebranding: "Myseum Rebrands as Myseum.AI to Align with Full Suite of Technology and Social Media Platforms", "XYZ Corp Changes Name to XYZ.AI, Announces Strategic Pivot to Artificial Intelligence", "Company Completes Corporate Rebrand to Reflect AI-First Strategy", "Acme Becomes Acme.AI, Effective Immediately", "Unveils New Corporate Identity as Quantum Logic Inc"
  Examples that are NOT ai_rebranding: "Announces AI Strategy" (no identity change), "Launches AI Division" (product_launch or ai_breakthrough), "Plans to Rename Company" (future, not completed), "Adds .AI to Product Line" (product, not corporate rebrand)
- clinical_breakthrough: Phase 2 or later clinical trial with EXCEPTIONAL positive results for a MAJOR, WIDELY-KNOWN disease. REQUIRES ALL of:
  (1) Phase 2 or Phase 3 (NOT Phase 1 — too early, safety-only)
  (2) Concrete positive outcome: met primary endpoint, statistically significant results, superiority over standard of care, OR strong response rates with specific numbers
  (3) Disease MUST be on this list OR be an equally famous, widely-feared disease you are ABSOLUTELY CERTAIN would spark the same investor reaction: cancer (lung, breast, colorectal, prostate, pancreatic, liver, ovarian, brain/glioblastoma, melanoma, leukemia, lymphoma), Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, ALS, obesity/GLP-1, diabetes, NASH/MASH, heart failure, MS, lupus, rheumatoid arthritis, sickle cell, muscular dystrophy, epinephrine delivery, anaphylaxis
  CRITICAL: The bar is "household name disease that the general public knows and fears." If you have to think about whether it qualifies, it doesn't — use clinical_trial_positive. Examples of diseases that do NOT qualify: back pain, COPD, eczema, rare/orphan diseases, genetic disorders not listed above. These may still be clinical_trial_positive — just not clinical_breakthrough.
  MUST NOT be clinical_breakthrough if: Phase 1, safety/tolerability only, extension study, preclinical/in vitro, "potential"/"may"/"could", trial initiation with no results, vague "positive trends" without specifics, disease not on the exact list above, post-hoc analysis or subgroup reanalysis of previously released data, publication of previously announced results in a journal (the data was already released — publication is not a new catalyst)
  Examples that ARE clinical_breakthrough: "Phase 2 Study Shows Statistically Significant Tumor Reduction in Colorectal Cancer", "Phase 3 Trial Meets Primary Endpoint in Alzheimer's", "Phase 2 Achieves Superior Epinephrine Delivery vs EpiPen"
  Examples that are NOT (use clinical_trial_positive): "Phase 1 Safety Data Shows Favorable Profile", "Phase 2 Shows Encouraging Trends", "Positive Data in Rare Enzyme Deficiency" (niche), "Initiates Phase 3 Trial" (no results), "Phase 2 Demonstrates Meaningful Improvements in Chronic Lumbar Disc Disease" (not on disease list), "Phase 3 Meets Endpoint in COPD" (not on disease list)
- cancer_catalyst: Cancer/oncology headline with concrete positive developments and named specifics, but does NOT meet the stricter clinical_breakthrough criteria (Phase 2+ met primary endpoint). REQUIRES ALL of: (1) Cancer or specific oncology disease named (lung, breast, colorectal, prostate, pancreatic, liver, ovarian, glioblastoma, melanoma, leukemia, lymphoma, etc.), (2) Named drug, compound, therapy, diagnostic platform, or specific mechanism (checkpoint inhibitor, ADC, CAR-T, etc.), (3) Concrete positive development: patent granted/approved, positive clinical data (any phase with specifics), novel mechanism partnership with named partner, regulatory milestone, OR third-party validation study from a prestigious institution (Dana-Farber, MD Anderson, Mayo Clinic, Memorial Sloan Kettering, Johns Hopkins, NCI) demonstrating value/efficacy/predictive value of the company's technology. MUST NOT be cancer_catalyst if: trial initiation with no results, preclinical/in vitro, "potential"/"may"/"could" without concrete outcome, safety/tolerability only, vague "cancer research" without named drug or specifics. If headline qualifies for clinical_breakthrough, use clinical_breakthrough instead (higher priority).
  Examples that ARE cancer_catalyst: "AIM ImmunoTech Granted New Patent for Ampligen Use in Pancreatic Cancer Treatment", "CytomX Therapeutics Reports Positive Phase 1b Data for CX-904 ADC in Solid Tumors With Named Tumor Types", "Dana-Farber Study Demonstrates Predictive Value of RPPA Platform for Enhertu Therapy in Metastatic Breast Cancer" (prestigious institution + named technology + named drug + cancer type)
  Examples that are NOT cancer_catalyst (classify as clinical_trial_positive or other): "Initiates Phase 1 Cancer Trial" (no results), "Potential Cancer Treatment Shows Promise" (vague), "Preclinical Data Shows Anti-Tumor Activity" (preclinical), "Announces Cancer Research Collaboration" (no named drug or specifics)
- partnership: A NEW formal cooperative arrangement between TWO NAMED entities with concrete scope. The SUBSTANCE is what matters: any principle-equivalent framing qualifies — whether the headline calls it a partnership, collaboration, alliance, joint venture, tie-up, "teaming up with", "joins forces with", "works with [named partner] to", "strategic relationship with", or any synonym describing formalized cooperation. Do NOT require specific noun like "partnership" — the economic event is "two named entities formalizing cooperation with a concrete scope", and any phrasing of that qualifies.
  REQUIRES: (1) both parties named (not "major partner" or "leading company"), AND (2) at least ONE of: specific dollar amount, specific product/service/technology named, specific market/application described. Vague cooperation announcements without specifics = "other". If the cooperation is specifically about AI/robotics/ML and meets ai_breakthrough PATH B criteria, use ai_breakthrough instead. If the cooperation has a specific dollar contract value or involves a household-name counterparty with revenue flowing to the subject company, consider major_contract instead.
  Examples that ARE partnership: "Partners With Microsoft to Deploy Cloud Analytics Platform" (named partner + specific product), "Enters Strategic Alliance With Pfizer for Drug Co-Development" (named + specific scope), "Teams Up With NVIDIA on GPU-Accelerated Analytics Pipeline" (named + specific technology), "Joins Forces With Siemens to Build Industrial IoT Platform" (named + specific scope)
  Examples that are NOT partnership (classify as "other"): "Announces Strategic Partnership" (unnamed partner), "Partners With Leading Company for Innovation" (no specifics), "Signs Partnership Agreement" (no details), "Teams Up With Industry Leader" (unnamed)
- licensing_agreement: A NEW formal transfer of rights to use, exploit, or commercialize a specific named technology, IP, drug, platform, or other asset, in exchange for revenue or reciprocal access. The SUBSTANCE is what matters: any principle-equivalent arrangement qualifies — whether the headline calls it a license, licensing agreement, rights grant, IP transfer, technology transfer, "grants exclusive rights to", "assigns rights for", "out-licenses", "in-licenses", or any synonym describing the transfer of usage/exploitation rights. Do NOT require the specific word "license" — the economic event is "one party grants another the right to use a specific named asset in exchange for consideration", and any phrasing of that qualifies. REQUIRES: named counterparty OR specific dollar amount OR named technology/IP. Vague rights arrangements without any of these = "other".
  PRIORITY ROUTING TO major_contract: If the licensing deal includes a SPECIFIC DOLLAR AMOUNT (upfront payment, deal value, or total contract value — not just "royalties" or "milestone payments") AND at least ONE of: (1) named counterparty, (2) named product/technology/IP, (3) specific addressable market size — classify as major_contract instead. The dollar amount makes it functionally a major contract regardless of legal structure. The more named entities and concrete figures in the headline, the stronger the case for major_contract.
  Examples that route to major_contract: "Enters $15M Licensing Agreement With Pfizer for Cancer Drug Platform" → major_contract (dollar amount + named counterparty + named product), "Signs $50M License Deal for Proprietary Battery Technology in $2B EV Market" → major_contract (dollar amount + named technology + market size), "Licenses Diabetes Drug to Novo Nordisk for $30M Upfront Plus Milestones" → major_contract (dollar amount + named counterparty + named product)
  Examples that stay licensing_agreement: "Receives Technology License from Qualcomm" → licensing_agreement (no dollar amount), "Enters Licensing Arrangement for Undisclosed Terms" → other (vague), "Licenses Drug Compound to Roche for Royalties" → licensing_agreement (no upfront dollar amount)
- fda_approval: FDA grants FULL APPROVAL (not clearance) for a drug, biologic, or medical product. Look for: "FDA approves", "receives FDA approval", "granted FDA approval". This is the strongest regulatory catalyst.
- fda_clearance: FDA grants 510(k) clearance or De Novo clearance for a medical device. Look for: "FDA clears", "receives 510(k) clearance", "granted FDA clearance". Different from approval — clearance is for devices, approval is for drugs.
- regulatory_approval: Non-FDA regulatory approvals from other agencies (EMA, Health Canada, PMDA, CE marking, DEA scheduling, USDA). Also covers significant government permits, licenses, or designations that unlock new revenue. NOT routine compliance certifications (SOC2, ISO, FedRAMP alone).
  Examples: "Receives EMA Approval for Cancer Drug" → regulatory_approval, "Granted DEA Schedule II Manufacturing License" → regulatory_approval
  NOT regulatory_approval: "Achieves SOC2 Compliance" → other, "Receives ISO Certification" → other
- grant_funding: Company receives a NEW grant, award, or non-dilutive funding from a government agency, foundation, or research body. REQUIRES: specific dollar amount OR named granting body. The key distinction from government_contract is that grants fund RESEARCH/DEVELOPMENT (no deliverable), while contracts fund PRODUCTS/SERVICES (specific deliverable). If unsure, prefer government_contract for awards with specific deliverables.
  Examples: "Awarded $5M NIH Grant for Alzheimer's Research" → grant_funding, "Receives $2M BARDA Funding for Vaccine Development" → grant_funding
  NOT grant_funding: "Wins $10M Army Contract for Drone Delivery" → government_contract (specific deliverable)
- patent_granted: Company is GRANTED or ISSUED a patent (not filed, not pending, not applied for). Must be a final grant/issuance. "Patent allowance" alone = "other" (not final). "Files patent" = "other". Only the actual grant matters.
  Examples: "Granted U.S. Patent for Novel Drug Delivery System" → patent_granted, "Issued Patent Covering AI-Based Diagnostic Method" → patent_granted
  NOT patent_granted: "Files Patent Application" → other, "Receives Patent Allowance" → other (not final grant)
- facility_expansion: Company opens, builds, or significantly expands a physical facility (factory, warehouse, data center, lab). This is an operational milestone — NOT a contract. Classify here when the headline is about the facility itself, not about a contract to build it.
- store_expansion: Retailer opens new stores or enters new geographic markets with physical locations.
- drill_results_positive: Mining or oil/gas company reports positive drilling, assay, or exploration results. Look for: grade/thickness of mineralization, flow rates, resource estimates. "Intersects 50m of 2.5 g/t gold" = drill_results_positive. Vague "exploration update" without results = other.
- clinical_trial_positive: Any clinical trial with positive results that does NOT meet the stricter clinical_breakthrough criteria. Includes: Phase 1 with favorable safety, Phase 2 in non-major diseases, positive data without meeting primary endpoint, any encouraging clinical data.
- clinical_trial_negative: Clinical trial with negative, disappointing, or failed results. Look for: "fails to meet endpoint", "did not achieve", "discontinued", "negative results", "safety concerns". This is a SKIP type — the postfilter blocks it.
- earnings_beat: Company reports earnings/revenue that BEAT analyst estimates. Look for: "beats", "tops estimates", "exceeds expectations", "better than expected".
- earnings_miss: Company reports earnings/revenue that MISS estimates. Look for: "misses", "falls short", "below expectations", "disappointing results". This is a SKIP type — the postfilter blocks it.
- guidance_raise: Company raises forward guidance. Look for: "raises guidance", "increases outlook", "upgrades forecast".
- guidance_cut: Company lowers forward guidance. Look for: "cuts guidance", "lowers outlook", "reduces forecast". This is a SKIP type — the postfilter blocks it.
- analyst_upgrade: Analyst or firm upgrades the stock rating. Look for: "upgraded", "initiates with Buy", "raises to Outperform".
- analyst_downgrade: Analyst or firm downgrades the stock rating. Look for: "downgraded", "cuts to Sell", "lowers to Underperform". This is a SKIP type — the postfilter blocks it.
- debt_restructuring: Company borrows, refinances, or reorganizes debt — receives non-dilutive CAPITAL (loans, credit facilities, term loans, revolving credit, debt refinancing, capital from named lenders such as Blue Owl, Apollo, Ares, Blackstone). The SUBSTANCE is what matters: any principle-equivalent event where the company is BORROWING rather than EARNING qualifies — whether called debt restructuring, credit facility, refinancing, non-dilutive capital raise, or any synonym. Distinct from major_contract (company EARNING revenue from products/services) — here money flows INTO the company as a loan. Healthier terms or lower rates are modestly bullish; defaults, emergency financing, or Chapter 11 filings are bearish and route to "other".
  Examples that ARE debt_restructuring: "Secures $500M in Non-Dilutive Capital from Blue Owl", "Refinances $200M Term Loan at Lower Rate", "Enters $150M Revolving Credit Facility with JPMorgan"
  Examples that are NOT debt_restructuring: "Adds $2.6M in Contracted Non-Dilutive Income" → major_contract (earning, not borrowing), "Defaults on Bond Payment" → other (bearish), "Files for Chapter 11" → other
- fda_designation: FDA grants a formal DESIGNATION that gives the company regulatory advantages but is NOT an approval. This is distinct from fda_approval (which is full marketing authorization) and from regulatory_approval (which is non-FDA). Designations unlock faster review, longer exclusivity, priority review vouchers, or similar benefits. REQUIRES: explicit designation-granting language AND a named designation type.
  Qualifying designation types: Fast Track Designation, Breakthrough Therapy Designation, Priority Review, Orphan Drug Designation, Rare Pediatric Disease Designation (RPDD), RMAT (Regenerative Medicine Advanced Therapy), Accelerated Approval pathway granted, Qualified Infectious Disease Product (QIDP), Emergency Use Authorization (EUA — for new indications, not ongoing).
  NOT fda_designation: IND clearance (permission to start trials, not a designation — use "other"), FDA meetings scheduled or granted (procedural — use "other"), BLA/NDA acceptance (use pivotal_trial_data or fda_approval depending on context), label expansion requests (use "other").
  Examples that ARE fda_designation: "FDA Grants Rare Pediatric Disease Designation to XYZ-001", "Receives Fast Track Designation for Parkinson's Program", "Awarded Breakthrough Therapy Designation for ALS Candidate", "FDA Grants Orphan Drug Designation for Rare Cancer Drug"
  Examples that are NOT fda_designation: "FDA Clears IND Application" → other (IND is permission, not designation), "FDA Meeting Scheduled" → other (procedural), "Receives 510(k) Clearance" → fda_clearance (device clearance)
- pivotal_trial_data: Phase 3, pivotal, or registrational clinical trial results — the data that typically supports an FDA filing. Distinct from clinical_trial_positive (broader, all phases) and clinical_breakthrough (requires household-name disease). Use pivotal_trial_data when the headline names Phase 3 / pivotal / registrational / confirmatory AND reports actual data (not trial initiation, not interim-no-numbers). This is the strongest clinical data catalyst short of full approval.
  REQUIRES: (1) explicit pivotal / Phase 3 / registrational / confirmatory language, AND (2) concrete result (met primary endpoint, statistical significance, specific efficacy number, superiority, overall survival benefit, objective response rate, etc.).
  Generalization: if the headline describes late-stage confirmatory human data with a clear positive outcome on a named drug, trust that as pivotal_trial_data even if the exact word "pivotal" is missing — the intent is late-stage confirmatory evidence.
  If the headline ALSO satisfies clinical_breakthrough (household-name disease like cancer, Alzheimer's, obesity, etc.), prefer clinical_breakthrough. pivotal_trial_data is for less-famous indications where the PHASE is the signal.
  Examples that ARE pivotal_trial_data: "Phase 3 RASolute 301 Shows Unprecedented Overall Survival Benefit for KRAS-Mutant Pancreatic Cancer" (could also be clinical_breakthrough — pick breakthrough), "Pivotal Trial Meets Primary Endpoint in Dry Eye Disease", "Registrational Study Achieves 62% Response Rate in Relapsed Lymphoma", "Phase 3 Confirmatory Trial Demonstrates Statistical Significance for IPF"
  Examples that are NOT pivotal_trial_data: "Phase 2 Data Shows Promise" → clinical_trial_positive, "Initiates Phase 3 Trial" → other (initiation, no data), "Phase 3 Interim Update" without numbers → other, "Positive Phase 3 Results" without any specifics → other (no data given).
- peer_reviewed_publication: A peer-reviewed journal publication that validates the COMPANY'S drug, platform, technology, biomarker, or mechanism with specific results. This is third-party scientific validation and is tradeable even when the company is small. REQUIRES: (1) explicit publication language ("publication in", "peer-reviewed", "published study", "journal article"), AND (2) a named asset belonging to the company (drug, platform, technology, product), AND (3) a positive outcome claim (demonstrates, validates, supports, confirms, predicts, reduces, improves).
  Generalization: the journal does NOT need to be Nature/NEJM/Lancet — any peer-reviewed journal counts as long as the asset is NAMED and the result is POSITIVE. Micro-caps rarely publish in top-tier journals; their catalysts often appear in specialty or disease-specific journals.
  Institutional attribution (Dana-Farber, MD Anderson, Mayo Clinic, NIH, Memorial Sloan Kettering, Johns Hopkins, named university) strengthens the signal but is not required.
  NOT peer_reviewed_publication: mechanistic papers (pharmacokinetics, receptor binding, in vitro selectivity) — route to "other"; a publication that MERELY MENTIONS the company without describing a validating result; preprint / non-peer-reviewed; publication of previously released clinical data in journal form (the data was already priced in — route to "other").
  Examples that ARE peer_reviewed_publication: "Peer-Reviewed Publication Supporting FABP5 Inhibitor ART26.12 as a Novel Pain Therapy", "Nexalin Announces Peer-Reviewed Study Showing DIFS Technology Reduced Self-Injurious Behavior", "Dana-Farber Study Demonstrates Predictive Value of Ignite Proteomics' RPPA Platform for Enhertu", "JAMA Publishes Positive Results for Company's Novel Biomarker Panel"
  Examples that are NOT peer_reviewed_publication: "Publication Explores Mechanism of Drug X" → other (mechanistic, not validation), "Company's Drug Mentioned in Journal Review" → other (no result), "Announces Publication of Pharmacokinetic Data" → other (PK, not efficacy), "Journal Publishes Previously Released Phase 2 Data" → other (already priced in)
- reimbursement_pathway: Company secures a reimbursement pathway, coverage code, or payor access that unlocks revenue for an existing product or service. These are catalysts because they translate regulatory approval into actual billable revenue. REQUIRES: a named reimbursement mechanism or coverage body AND a named company product/test/therapy.
  Qualifying mechanisms: AMA CPT/PLA code granted, CMS National Coverage Determination (NCD) positive, CMS Local Coverage Determination (LCD) positive, Medicare reimbursement rate set, Medicaid coverage approved, NICE positive guidance (UK), HTA positive recommendation, ICD reimbursement pathway, commercial payor policy coverage for the company's test.
  NOT reimbursement_pathway: generic "insurance coverage" announcements without a named mechanism, routine billing code assignments with no economic impact, coverage being denied or withdrawn (use "other").
  Examples that ARE reimbursement_pathway: "Receives AMA PLA Code for CNSide CSF Tumor Cell Enumeration Test", "Obtains CMS National Coverage Determination for Liquid Biopsy Test", "NICE Issues Positive Final Draft Guidance for Drug X", "Medicare Finalizes Reimbursement Rate for Diagnostic Panel"
  Examples that are NOT reimbursement_pathway: "Test Now Available to Insured Patients" → other (no named mechanism), "Payor Denies Coverage" → other (negative), "Files for Medicare Coverage" → other (filing, not granted)
- listing_compliance_regained: Company RESOLVES a prior exchange listing deficiency (typically Nasdaq or NYSE minimum bid price, minimum publicly held shares, or stockholders' equity) — removing immediate delisting risk. The SUBSTANCE is what matters: any principle-equivalent resolution qualifies — whether the headline uses "regains compliance", "compliance regained", "returns to compliance", "restored compliance", "back in compliance", "deficiency resolved", "cured deficiency", "exchange lifts deficiency notice", or any synonym describing the resolution of a listing-rule problem. Do NOT require the exact phrase "regains compliance". REQUIRES: (1) an identifiable prior deficiency that is now resolved or extended, AND (2) a named exchange/rule.
  Also qualifies: extension granted to regain compliance (still removes immediate delisting, though slightly weaker).
  NOT listing_compliance_regained: initial deficiency notices (bearish — use "other"), appeals pending (not resolved — use "other"), delisting confirmed (bearish — use "other").
  Examples that ARE listing_compliance_regained: "Regains Compliance with Nasdaq Minimum Bid Price Requirement", "Regains Compliance with Nasdaq's Minimum Publicly Held Shares Requirement", "Granted 180-Day Extension by Nasdaq to Regain Compliance"
  Examples that are NOT: "Receives Delisting Notice" → other, "Appeals Nasdaq Delisting Determination" → other, "Announces 1-for-60 Reverse Stock Split to Regain Compliance" → other (the split itself is the event, not compliance regain yet)
- strategic_investment_received: Company RECEIVES a strategic investment from a named counterparty, typically at PREMIUM or market price (not a discounted dilutive offering). Distinct from offering_announced: offering_announced = raising cash from public markets (dilutive, usually at discount); strategic_investment_received = strategic counterparty putting capital in as part of a broader relationship, often at or above market. REQUIRES: (1) a named strategic investor OR clearly strategic framing (industrial partner, sovereign fund, strategic investment framework), AND (2) either a dollar amount, a "premium to market" phrase, or a concrete strategic rationale (collaboration, commercial tie-in, defensive alliance). Generic "private placement"/"PIPE" without strategic rationale, "at-the-market offering", and "registered direct offering" all → offering_announced (public market mechanisms).
  Examples that ARE strategic_investment_received: "Enters Non-Binding Term Sheet for $17.5M Strategic Investment at Significant Premium", "Secures Institutional Investment Framework Supporting Up to $150 Million Investment", "Announces $50M Strategic Investment from KCGI", "Strategic Investor Takes Minority Stake at 30% Premium"
- strategic_alternatives_review: Company announces a FORMAL board-initiated process to evaluate the sale, merger, or other transformational transaction — often a precursor to being sold, merged, or taken private. This is a bullish M&A signal for the company running the process. The SUBSTANCE is what matters: any principle-equivalent framing qualifies — whether the headline uses "strategic alternatives", "strategic review", "process to maximize shareholder value", "formal sale process", "engages banker to explore options", "board-initiated review of options", or any synonym describing a formal board-sanctioned process to evaluate transformational outcomes. Do NOT require the exact phrase "strategic alternatives". REQUIRES: (1) evidence of a FORMAL process (not casual exec commentary), shown by at least ONE of: (a) investment bank engaged, (b) unsolicited interest received, (c) formal board process disclosed, (d) explicit "process", "review", or "exploration" language coupled with an M&A/sale outcome implied.
  Strength is higher when a named investment bank is listed (Goldman, Lazard, Roth Capital, Raymond James, Oppenheimer, etc.) or when unsolicited expressions of interest are mentioned.
  NOT strategic_alternatives_review: defensive "exploring strategic options" language in an earnings call without any process detail → other; vague "considering alternatives" without a bank or process → other; confirming a previously announced process continuing → other.
  Examples that ARE strategic_alternatives_review: "Announces Review of Strategic Alternatives to Maximize Shareholder Value", "Engages Goldman Sachs to Review Unsolicited Interest and Evaluate Strategic Alternatives", "Retains Roth Capital to Explore Strategic Alternatives", "Board Receives Unsolicited Acquisition Proposal and Initiates Strategic Review"
  Examples that are NOT: "Exploring Strategic Options for Growth" (earnings context, no process) → other, "Continues to Evaluate Strategic Direction" → other (vague, no bank, no process)
- revenue_milestone: Company announces a SPECIFIC revenue milestone with concrete numbers. Distinct from earnings_beat (quarterly reporting vs estimates) and from vague "record revenue" claims. Two patterns qualify:

  PATTERN A — ANNUALIZED / ARR MILESTONE: a specific revenue figure AND a forward-looking framing (run rate, ARR, all-time record with growth %, tier crossed). The "we just hit $X ARR" pattern common for growth companies between earnings releases.

  PATTERN B — EVENT / PERIOD / PRODUCT RECORD: a specific $ figure from a named event, trade show, product launch, rollout, or time period, combined with a MATERIALITY QUALIFIER ("record-breaking", "all-time record", "landmark", "best-ever"). The annualized vs event-based distinction matters less than the materiality of the figure relative to company size. Small / micro-cap companies announcing record revenues from a specific event or channel often move hard — the market doesn't distinguish "annualized" from "event-based" when the $ figure is large relative to market cap or is the first time the company has reported such a number. Self-declared "record-breaking" is a valid materiality signal because companies deliberately use those words when the result IS material.

  NOT revenue_milestone: quarterly earnings reports (use earnings_beat), vague "record revenue" without ANY specific figure → other, retrospective "returned to growth" framing without a number → other.

  Examples that ARE revenue_milestone:
    "Announces $12 Million Annualized Revenue Run Rate" → Pattern A (ARR)
    "Crosses $50M ARR Threshold" → Pattern A (tier crossed)
    "Hits All-Time Record Quarterly Revenue of $25M, Up 146% YoY" → Pattern A (record + growth %)
    "Announces Record-Breaking Performance at NATAS Fair with Over US$3.34 Million in Travel Bookings" → Pattern B (specific $ + "record-breaking" materiality qualifier)
    "Reports All-Time Record Single-Day Sales of $8.2M on Cyber Monday" → Pattern B (specific $ + all-time record framing from a named event)
    "Landmark $15M Q4 Product Launch Revenue Exceeds 2024 Full-Year Total" → Pattern B (specific $ + "landmark" qualifier + size framing)

  Examples that are NOT: "Reports Record Revenue" → other (no number), "Q1 Results Show Strong Growth" → earnings_beat (quarterly report), "Revenue Up 15% YoY" → other (modest growth, no milestone framing)

Rules:

PRIORITY RULE — ACQUISITION OVERRIDES EVERYTHING:
If the headline contains ANY acquisition language ("acquires", "acquisition of", "to acquire", "re-activates acquisition", "closes acquisition", "signs LOI to acquire", "secures ownership of", "completes purchase of"), it MUST be classified as acquisition_announced or acquisition_target — NEVER as major_contract, government_contract, defense_order, or any other type, regardless of revenue targets, dollar amounts, defense context, or any other details in the headline. An acquisition is a CASH OUTFLOW (company spending money to buy another entity) — it is fundamentally different from a contract (company receiving money for services). Check for acquisition language FIRST before considering any other classification.

CASH FLOW DIRECTION PRINCIPLE:
We only trade events where the company RECEIVES value (cash inflow, achievement, approval). Events where the company SPENDS money (acquisitions, investments outward, facility purchases) are cash outflows and bearish. When in doubt about a headline, ask: "Is the company RECEIVING money/value, or SPENDING money?" If spending, it should be acquisition_announced, offering_announced, or other — never a tradeable contract type.

- government_contract: ANY government deal (federal, state, NASA, DARPA, DoD, agency awards)
- military_contract: ANY military/defense deal (Army, Navy, Air Force, Space Force, foreign military sales, weapons, drones, munitions, counter-UAS, satellites for defense)
- defense_order: orders from defense companies or for defense products (not government directly)
- major_contract: NEW commercial contracts/orders/agreements that are CONCRETE and MATERIAL. REQUIRES at least ONE of: (1) specific dollar amount, (2) named well-known counterparty (Fortune 500, major institution, government entity), (3) government/military order. Must be revenue-generating for the company (company RECEIVING money for products/services delivered). EXCLUDE: contract renewals/extensions, PRV sales, royalty agreements, merger/acquisition agreements, FDA pathway agreements, vague "strategic" partnerships without $ amounts, company SPENDING money (acquisitions, investments outward), vague deployments/partnerships with unnamed customers, and ALL financing/lending/capital arrangements — non-dilutive capital, credit facilities, term loans, revolving credit, debt financing, capital raises from financial firms (e.g. Blue Owl, Apollo, Ares, Blackstone lending), share repurchase program announcements. KEY: if the headline has NO dollar amount AND NO named counterparty AND is NOT a government order, it is NOT major_contract — classify as "other". KEY: if the company is BORROWING money or receiving a LOAN/CREDIT LINE (even with a large $ amount and named lender), it is NOT major_contract — classify as "debt_restructuring" or "stock_buyback" as appropriate. KEY: if the headline mentions "acquisition of" or "acquires" ANYWHERE, it is NEVER major_contract — always use acquisition_announced or acquisition_target (see PRIORITY RULE above).
  Examples that ARE major_contract: "Secures $15M Contract With Fortune 500", "Wins $8M Order From U.S. Army" (also military_contract), "Signs $20M Deal With Microsoft"
  Examples that are NOT major_contract (classify as "other" or appropriate type): "Secures New Multifamily Deployment" (no $ amount, no named customer), "Announces New Enterprise Partnership With Prominent Company" (unnamed = not credible), "Expands Commercial Rollout" (vague operational milestone), "Secures New Customer Deployment" (no specifics), "Secures $500M in Non-Dilutive Capital from Blue Owl" (financing/lending, not revenue — use debt_restructuring), "Secures Credit Facility With Goldman Sachs" (loan, not contract — use debt_restructuring), "Announces $300M Share Repurchase Program" (buyback, not contract — use stock_buyback), "Re-Activates 70% Acquisition of Tekne, Advancing Defense Platform Targeting €100M+ Revenue" (ACQUISITION — use acquisition_announced, the €100M is a future revenue target, NOT a contract won)
- acquisition_target: the company named FIRST in the headline is BEING acquired/bought. Look for: "X to be acquired", "X agrees to be acquired", "X enters agreement to be acquired", "Y to acquire X" (X is target), "tender offer for X", "X receives buyout offer". Also: if headline says "Y and X announce acquisition" and context shows Y is buying X, classify as acquisition_target if X is the smaller/subject company being absorbed.
- acquisition_announced: the company named FIRST in the headline is DOING the acquiring/buying (spending money). Look for: "X acquires Y", "X signs LOI to acquire", "X closes acquisition of", "X to acquire Y", "X secures ownership of", "X re-activates acquisition of", "X completes purchase of", "X secures ownership of". The acquirer is SPENDING cash — this is bearish for the acquirer's stock. IMPORTANT: Even if the headline mentions revenue targets, defense context, dollar amounts, or other positive-sounding details, if the company is ACQUIRING another entity, it is ALWAYS acquisition_announced.
- ai_breakthrough: Headlines where AI/robotics/machine-learning is the CORE catalyst — not a buzzword. Two paths qualify:
  PATH A — PRODUCT LAUNCH: Company launches, deploys, or unveils a specific AI product, tool, agent, or platform. REQUIRES at least TWO of: (1) named AI product/tool/agent, (2) specific function it performs, (3) concrete results claim.
  PATH B — AI EXPANSION: Company announces a collaboration, partnership, or expansion that is SPECIFICALLY about building or deploying AI/robotics/ML capabilities. REQUIRES ALL of: (1) a named partner or counterparty, (2) a concrete AI/robotics domain (e.g. "AI tutoring", "robotics education", "ML drug discovery"), (3) clear growth signal (expand, launch, deploy, build, develop, roll out — NOT just "explore" or "evaluate").
  Examples that ARE ai_breakthrough: "Launches AI Agent Revolutionizing Biospecimen Matching" (Path A), "Deploys AI Drug Discovery Platform Achieving 95% Accuracy" (Path A), "Announces Collaboration With Walimaker to Expand AI and Robotics Learning Programs" (Path B — named partner + concrete AI domain + expansion), "Partners With NVIDIA to Build AI-Powered Medical Imaging Pipeline" (Path B), "Begins Collaboration With Tencent to Support AI Tutor Platform With Real-Time Voice" (Path B)
  Examples that are NOT ai_breakthrough (classify as "other" or "partnership"): "Announces AI-Powered Solutions" (no specifics), "Integrating AI Into Platform" (vague), "AI-Driven Revenue Growth" (marketing), "Partners With AI Company" (no domain specifics, unnamed capabilities), "Hiring Chief AI Officer" (staffing), "Exploring AI Opportunities" (no commitment), "Leveraging AI To Enhance Operations" (buzzword)
- clinical_breakthrough: Phase 2 or later clinical trial with EXCEPTIONAL positive results for a MAJOR, WIDELY-KNOWN disease. REQUIRES ALL of:
  (1) Phase 2 or Phase 3 (NOT Phase 1 — too early, safety-only)
  (2) Concrete positive outcome: met primary endpoint, statistically significant results, superiority over standard of care, OR strong response rates with specific numbers
  (3) Disease MUST be on this list OR be an equally famous, widely-feared disease you are ABSOLUTELY CERTAIN would spark the same investor reaction: cancer (lung, breast, colorectal, prostate, pancreatic, liver, ovarian, brain/glioblastoma, melanoma, leukemia, lymphoma), Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, ALS, obesity/GLP-1, diabetes, NASH/MASH, heart failure, MS, lupus, rheumatoid arthritis, sickle cell, muscular dystrophy, epinephrine delivery, anaphylaxis
  CRITICAL: The bar is "household name disease that the general public knows and fears." If you have to think about whether it qualifies, it doesn't — use clinical_trial_positive. Diseases that do NOT qualify include but are not limited to: back pain, lumbar disc disease, chronic pain, arthritis (non-RA), COPD, asthma, eczema, psoriasis, IBS, Crohn's, ulcerative colitis, kidney disease, liver fibrosis, eye diseases, hearing loss, rare/orphan diseases, genetic disorders not listed. These may still be clinical_trial_positive — just not clinical_breakthrough.
  MUST NOT be clinical_breakthrough if: Phase 1, safety/tolerability only, extension study, preclinical/in vitro, "potential"/"may"/"could", trial initiation with no results, vague "positive trends" without specifics, disease not on the exact list above
  Examples that ARE clinical_breakthrough: "Phase 2 Study Shows Statistically Significant Tumor Reduction in Colorectal Cancer", "Phase 3 Trial Meets Primary Endpoint in Alzheimer's", "Phase 2 Achieves Superior Epinephrine Delivery vs EpiPen"
  Examples that are NOT (use clinical_trial_positive): "Phase 1 Safety Data Shows Favorable Profile", "Phase 2 Shows Encouraging Trends", "Positive Data in Rare Enzyme Deficiency" (niche), "Initiates Phase 3 Trial" (no results), "Phase 2 Demonstrates Meaningful Improvements in Chronic Lumbar Disc Disease" (not on disease list), "Phase 3 Meets Endpoint in COPD" (not on disease list)
- cancer_catalyst: Cancer/oncology headline with concrete positive developments and named specifics, but does NOT meet the stricter clinical_breakthrough criteria (Phase 2+ met primary endpoint). REQUIRES ALL of: (1) Cancer or specific oncology disease named (lung, breast, colorectal, prostate, pancreatic, liver, ovarian, glioblastoma, melanoma, leukemia, lymphoma, etc.), (2) Named drug, compound, therapy, or specific mechanism (checkpoint inhibitor, ADC, CAR-T, etc.), (3) Concrete positive development: patent granted/approved, positive clinical data (any phase with specifics), novel mechanism partnership with named partner, regulatory milestone. MUST NOT be cancer_catalyst if: trial initiation with no results, preclinical/in vitro, "potential"/"may"/"could" without concrete outcome, safety/tolerability only, vague "cancer research" without named drug or specifics. If headline qualifies for clinical_breakthrough, use clinical_breakthrough instead (higher priority).
  Examples that ARE cancer_catalyst: "AIM ImmunoTech Granted New Patent for Ampligen Use in Pancreatic Cancer Treatment", "CytomX Therapeutics Reports Positive Phase 1b Data for CX-904 ADC in Solid Tumors With Named Tumor Types"
  Examples that are NOT cancer_catalyst (classify as clinical_trial_positive or other): "Initiates Phase 1 Cancer Trial" (no results), "Potential Cancer Treatment Shows Promise" (vague), "Preclinical Data Shows Anti-Tumor Activity" (preclinical), "Announces Cancer Research Collaboration" (no named drug or specifics)

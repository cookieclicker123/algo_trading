Rules:
- government_contract: ANY government deal (federal, state, NASA, DARPA, DoD, agency awards)
- military_contract: ANY military/defense deal (Army, Navy, Air Force, Space Force, foreign military sales, weapons, drones, munitions, counter-UAS, satellites for defense)
- defense_order: orders from defense companies or for defense products (not government directly)
- major_contract: NEW commercial contracts/orders/agreements that are CONCRETE and MATERIAL. REQUIRES at least ONE of: (1) specific dollar amount, (2) named well-known counterparty (Fortune 500, major institution, government entity), (3) government/military order. Must be revenue-generating for the company (company RECEIVING money). EXCLUDE: contract renewals/extensions, PRV sales, royalty agreements, merger/acquisition agreements, FDA pathway agreements, vague "strategic" partnerships without $ amounts, company SPENDING money (acquisitions, investments outward), and vague deployments/partnerships with unnamed customers. KEY: if the headline has NO dollar amount AND NO named counterparty AND is NOT a government order, it is NOT major_contract — classify as "other".
  Examples that ARE major_contract: "Secures $15M Contract With Fortune 500", "Wins $8M Order From U.S. Army" (also military_contract), "Signs $20M Deal With Microsoft"
  Examples that are NOT major_contract (classify as "other"): "Secures New Multifamily Deployment" (no $ amount, no named customer), "Announces New Enterprise Partnership With Prominent Company" (unnamed = not credible), "Expands Commercial Rollout" (vague operational milestone), "Secures New Customer Deployment" (no specifics)
- acquisition_target: the company named FIRST in the headline is BEING acquired/bought. Look for: "X to be acquired", "X agrees to be acquired", "X enters agreement to be acquired", "Y to acquire X" (X is target), "tender offer for X", "X receives buyout offer". Also: if headline says "Y and X announce acquisition" and context shows Y is buying X, classify as acquisition_target if X is the smaller/subject company being absorbed.
- acquisition_announced: the company named FIRST in the headline is DOING the acquiring/buying (spending money). Look for: "X acquires Y", "X signs LOI to acquire", "X closes acquisition of", "X to acquire Y", "X secures ownership of". The acquirer is SPENDING cash — this is bearish for the acquirer's stock.
- ai_breakthrough: Headlines where AI/robotics/machine-learning is the CORE catalyst — not a buzzword. Two paths qualify:
  PATH A — PRODUCT LAUNCH: Company launches, deploys, or unveils a specific AI product, tool, agent, or platform. REQUIRES at least TWO of: (1) named AI product/tool/agent, (2) specific function it performs, (3) concrete results claim.
  PATH B — AI EXPANSION: Company announces a collaboration, partnership, or expansion that is SPECIFICALLY about building or deploying AI/robotics/ML capabilities. REQUIRES ALL of: (1) a named partner or counterparty, (2) a concrete AI/robotics domain (e.g. "AI tutoring", "robotics education", "ML drug discovery"), (3) clear growth signal (expand, launch, deploy, build, develop, roll out — NOT just "explore" or "evaluate").
  Examples that ARE ai_breakthrough: "Launches AI Agent Revolutionizing Biospecimen Matching" (Path A), "Deploys AI Drug Discovery Platform Achieving 95% Accuracy" (Path A), "Announces Collaboration With Walimaker to Expand AI and Robotics Learning Programs" (Path B — named partner + concrete AI domain + expansion), "Partners With NVIDIA to Build AI-Powered Medical Imaging Pipeline" (Path B), "Begins Collaboration With Tencent to Support AI Tutor Platform With Real-Time Voice" (Path B)
  Examples that are NOT ai_breakthrough (classify as "other" or "partnership"): "Announces AI-Powered Solutions" (no specifics), "Integrating AI Into Platform" (vague), "AI-Driven Revenue Growth" (marketing), "Partners With AI Company" (no domain specifics, unnamed capabilities), "Hiring Chief AI Officer" (staffing), "Exploring AI Opportunities" (no commitment), "Leveraging AI To Enhance Operations" (buzzword)
- clinical_breakthrough: Phase 2 or later clinical trial with EXCEPTIONAL positive results for a MAJOR disease. REQUIRES ALL of:
  (1) Phase 2 or Phase 3 (NOT Phase 1 — too early, safety-only)
  (2) Concrete positive outcome: met primary endpoint, statistically significant results, superiority over standard of care, OR strong response rates with specific numbers
  (3) Major disease or known therapeutic area: cancer (lung, breast, colorectal, prostate, pancreatic, liver, ovarian, brain/glioblastoma, melanoma, leukemia, lymphoma), Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, ALS, obesity/GLP-1, diabetes, NASH/MASH, heart failure, MS, lupus, rheumatoid arthritis, sickle cell, muscular dystrophy, epinephrine delivery, anaphylaxis
  MUST NOT be clinical_breakthrough if: Phase 1, safety/tolerability only, extension study, preclinical/in vitro, "potential"/"may"/"could", trial initiation with no results, vague "positive trends" without specifics, unknown/niche disease with no public awareness
  Examples that ARE clinical_breakthrough: "Phase 2 Study Shows Statistically Significant Tumor Reduction in Colorectal Cancer", "Phase 3 Trial Meets Primary Endpoint in Alzheimer's", "Phase 2 Achieves Superior Epinephrine Delivery vs EpiPen"
  Examples that are NOT (use clinical_trial_positive): "Phase 1 Safety Data Shows Favorable Profile", "Phase 2 Shows Encouraging Trends", "Positive Data in Rare Enzyme Deficiency" (niche), "Initiates Phase 3 Trial" (no results)
- If unsure between military_contract and government_contract, prefer military_contract for anything involving armed forces, weapons, or defense systems

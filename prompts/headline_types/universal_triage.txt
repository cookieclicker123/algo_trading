Rules:

PRINCIPLE-FIRST RULE — THE NATURE OF THE EVENT MATTERS, NOT THE WORDING (APPLIES TO EVERY TYPE):
For every type below, the nouns, verbs, and phrasings shown in examples are ILLUSTRATIVE, not exhaustive. Your job is to recognize the underlying ECONOMIC PRINCIPLE of the headline, then classify it based on what is happening — not which specific word the company used to describe it. A "program" at a major retailer, a "rollout", a "supply arrangement", a "win", a "selection", and a "deal" are all the SAME economic event as a "contract" when the substance is the same: the company receives new revenue-generating business from a named counterparty. The same principle holds for every other type — product launches, partnerships, approvals, clinical data, licensing, designations, and so on. Do not require the exact nouns or verbs shown in examples. If a headline is PRINCIPLE-EQUIVALENT to an example (same economic nature, different wording), classify it the same type. Trust your understanding of the event. Overfitting to specific words causes you to miss the same catalyst dressed in different language. The listed terms and examples are anchors, not a closed set — synonyms and re-wordings of the same principle always count.

UNIVERSAL RULE — NEW ANNOUNCEMENTS ONLY (APPLIES TO EVERY TYPE):
We ONLY trade NEW, FORWARD-LOOKING events the market has not yet priced in. If a headline REPORTS on something that ALREADY HAPPENED, it is NOT tradeable — classify as "other" regardless of how bullish the event sounds. The market prices in events at announcement time; backward-looking reports are not catalysts.
  PAST-TENSE RED FLAGS: "achieved", "completed", "has repurchased", "returned $X to shareholders", "bought back", "previously", "last quarter", "during Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4", "year-to-date results", "delivered on", "fulfilled". These describe what already happened — NOT a new event.
  FORWARD-LOOKING GREEN FLAGS: "announces", "launches", "commences", "authorizes", "awarded", "wins", "enters into", "signs", "initiates", "receives approval", "grants". These describe something happening NOW.
  Ask: "Is this headline telling the market something NEW it didn't know, or REPORTING on something that already happened?" If reporting → "other".

PRIORITY RULE — MERGER vs ACQUISITION (DIFFERENT EVENTS):
A "Merger Agreement" between two companies is NOT an acquisition. In a merger, two companies COMBINE — neither is spending cash to buy the other. This is fundamentally different from "Company A acquires Company B" (cash outflow). Classify correctly:
  merger_agreement: "Announce Merger Agreement", "Enter Into Merger Agreement", "Agree to Merge", "Merger of Equals", "Sign Definitive Merger Agreement", "All-Stock Merger". Two named OPERATING COMPANIES combining.
  acquisition_announced: "Acquires", "To Acquire", "Closes Acquisition", "Signs LOI to Acquire", "Secures Ownership Of", "Completes Purchase Of". One company BUYING another — cash outflow, bearish for acquirer.
  acquisition_target: "To Be Acquired", "Agrees to Be Acquired", "Receives Buyout Offer". Company BEING bought — bullish.
  CRITICAL: "Merger Agreement" → merger_agreement. "Acquires" → acquisition_announced. Do NOT classify mergers as acquisitions.
  EXCLUSION — FUND/TRUST MERGERS ARE NOT merger_agreement:
  merger_agreement ONLY applies to OPERATING COMPANIES (companies that sell products or services) combining. The following are NOT merger_agreement — always classify as "other":
    (1) CLOSED-END FUND / INVESTMENT FUND / TRUST mergers: If either entity name contains "Fund", "Trust", "Income Fund", "Strategy Fund", "Capital Trust", "Investment Fund", or is clearly a closed-end fund, ETF, or investment vehicle, the merger is routine fund consolidation — NOT a catalytic event. Asset managers regularly merge funds to reduce overhead.
    (2) ETF mergers or reorganizations: ETFs merging, reorganizing, or converting between structures are administrative — NOT merger_agreement.
  Examples that are NOT merger_agreement (classify as "other"):
    "XYZ Income Fund Enters Into Merger Agreement with ABC Capital Trust" → other (fund merger)
    "AllianzGI Convertible & Income Fund Announces Merger with AllianzGI Diversified Income Fund" → other (fund consolidation)
    "BlackRock Municipal Trust Agrees to Merge with BlackRock Income Trust" → other (trust merger)
    "Eaton Vance Senior Floating-Rate Trust Merges Into Eaton Vance Income Fund" → other (fund reorganization)
  Examples that ARE merger_agreement:
    "SiriusXM and Liberty Media Sign Definitive Merger Agreement" → merger_agreement (NEW announcement, operating companies)
    "Sprint and T-Mobile Announce Merger of Equals" → merger_agreement (NEW announcement, operating companies)
  EXCLUSION — NON-CATALYST MERGER EVENTS ARE NOT merger_agreement:
  merger_agreement ONLY applies to the INITIAL ANNOUNCEMENT of a new merger between operating companies. The following are NOT merger_agreement — always classify as "other":
    (1) MERGER COMPLETION/CLOSING: The premium was priced in at announcement time. Completion is confirmation, not a new catalyst. ("Completes Merger", "Closes Merger Agreement", "Merger Finalized", "Merger Transaction Closes")
    (2) MERGER TERMINATION/WITHDRAWAL: NEGATIVE event — the opposite of a merger announcement. ("Terminates Merger Agreement", "Merger Called Off", "Withdraws from Merger", "Merger Agreement Abandoned", "Mutual Termination of Merger")
    (3) MERGER AMENDMENTS/EXTENSIONS: Modifications to an existing merger, not a new one. ("Amends Merger Agreement", "Extends Merger Deadline", "Revises Merger Terms", "Merger Consideration Adjusted")
    (4) REGULATORY/LEGAL CHALLENGES: Uncertainty or negative developments around existing mergers. ("FTC Challenges Merger", "DOJ Sues to Block Merger", "Shareholder Lawsuit Over Merger", "Proxy Advisor Recommends Against Merger", "Regulatory Review of Merger")
    (5) MERGER COMMENTARY/STATUS UPDATES: Status reports on already-announced mergers. ("Provides Update on Pending Merger", "Merger Progressing as Expected", "Reaffirms Commitment to Merger", "Expects Merger to Close in Q3")
  Examples that are NOT merger_agreement (classify as "other"):
    "Completes Previously Announced Merger with XYZ Corp" → other (completion, already priced in)
    "Terminates Merger Agreement with ABC Inc" → other (negative event)
    "Extends Merger Agreement Deadline to September" → other (amendment)
    "FTC Files Suit to Block Merger" → other (regulatory challenge)

PRIORITY RULE — ACQUISITION OVERRIDES EVERYTHING:
If the headline contains ANY acquisition language ("acquires", "acquisition of", "to acquire", "re-activates acquisition", "closes acquisition", "signs LOI to acquire", "secures ownership of", "completes purchase of"), it MUST be classified as acquisition_announced or acquisition_target — NEVER as major_contract, government_contract, defense_order, or any other type, regardless of revenue targets, dollar amounts, defense context, or any other details in the headline. An acquisition is a CASH OUTFLOW (company spending money to buy another entity) — it is fundamentally different from a contract (company receiving money for services). Check for acquisition language FIRST before considering any other classification. NOTE: "Merger Agreement" is NOT acquisition — see MERGER vs ACQUISITION rule above.

PRIORITY RULE — BUYBACK vs OFFERING (OPPOSITE EVENTS — DO NOT CONFUSE):
A "self tender offer to purchase shares" or "tender offer to purchase shares" = the company BUYING BACK its own stock = stock_buyback (BULLISH). An "offering" = the company SELLING new shares = offering_announced (BEARISH/dilutive). These are OPPOSITE events. The word "offer" appears in both but they mean completely different things. Check the DIRECTION: is the company PURCHASING/BUYING BACK shares (stock_buyback) or SELLING/ISSUING new shares (offering_announced)?
  stock_buyback: "Self Tender Offer To Purchase Shares", "Commences Tender Offer To Purchase Up To X Shares", "Announces Share Repurchase Program", "Board Authorizes Stock Buyback", "Modified Dutch Auction Tender Offer"
  offering_announced: "Prices Public Offering", "Announces Registered Direct Offering", "Prices Secondary Offering", "Closes Private Placement", "Enters Into Securities Purchase Agreement"
  CRITICAL: If the headline says "Tender Offer To PURCHASE" shares → stock_buyback. If it says "Offering OF shares" or "Prices Offering" → offering_announced. NEVER classify a share repurchase/buyback/tender-offer-to-purchase as offering_announced.
  EXCLUSION — BACKWARD-LOOKING BUYBACK REPORTS ARE NOT stock_buyback:
  stock_buyback requires a NEW, FORWARD-LOOKING announcement — the company is INITIATING or AUTHORIZING a buyback it has not done yet. If the headline REPORTS on buybacks that have ALREADY BEEN COMPLETED, it is NOT stock_buyback — classify as "other". The market prices in buybacks at announcement time; a report on past repurchase activity is not a catalyst. Look for past-tense or completion language: "achieved", "completed", "repurchased X shares during", "bought back", "has repurchased", "total shares repurchased", "repurchase activity", "returned $X to shareholders via buybacks". These describe what already happened — NOT a new program.
  Examples that are NOT stock_buyback (classify as "other"):
    "Achieved $50M in Share Repurchases During Q1" → other (backward-looking report)
    "Repurchased 2M Shares in Q4 Under Existing Program" → other (completed activity)
    "Returned $100M to Shareholders Through Share Buybacks" → other (past tense)
    "Completed Share Repurchase Program" → other (program already finished)
    "Has Repurchased $20M of Common Stock Year-to-Date" → other (status update)

  EXCLUSION — FUND/TRUST TENDER OFFERS ARE NOT stock_buyback:
  stock_buyback ONLY applies to OPERATING COMPANIES (companies that sell products or services) buying back their own COMMON stock. The following are NOT stock_buyback — always classify as "other":
    (1) CLOSED-END FUND / INVESTMENT FUND / TRUST tender offers: If the entity name contains "Fund", "Trust", "Capital Fund", "Income Fund", "Strategy Fund", "Investment Fund", or is clearly a closed-end fund or investment vehicle, its tender offer is routine NAV discount management — NOT a bullish catalyst. These funds regularly do conditional tender offers to narrow their NAV discount. This is housekeeping, not a signal of confidence.
    (2) PREFERRED SHARE tender offers: Tender offers for "preferred shares", "auction preferred shares", or any non-common-stock class are capital structure management — NOT stock_buyback.
    (3) CONDITIONAL tender offers from funds: "Board Determination to Proceed with Conditional Tender Offer" from a fund = "other".
  Examples that are NOT stock_buyback (classify as "other"):
    "Japan Smaller Capitalization Fund Announces Board Determination to Proceed with Conditional Tender Offer" → other (closed-end fund)
    "Virtus Dividend, Interest & Premium Strategy Fund Announces Tender Offer" → other (closed-end fund)
    "Eaton Vance Senior Income Trust Announce Tender Offers for Outstanding Auction Preferred Shares" → other (trust + preferred shares)
    "AllianzGI Convertible & Income Fund Announces Tender Offer" → other (closed-end fund)
    "BlackRock Capital Allocation Trust Commences Tender Offer" → other (investment trust)
  Examples that ARE stock_buyback:
    "Apple Announces $100B Share Repurchase Program" → stock_buyback (NEW announcement, operating company, common stock)
    "Tesla Commences Tender Offer To Purchase Up To 10M Shares" → stock_buyback (NEW initiation, operating company, common stock)
    "Board Authorizes $50M Stock Buyback" → stock_buyback (NEW authorization)

CASH FLOW DIRECTION PRINCIPLE:
We only trade events where the company RECEIVES value (cash inflow, achievement, approval). Events where the company SPENDS money (acquisitions, investments outward, facility purchases) are cash outflows and bearish. When in doubt about a headline, ask: "Is the company RECEIVING money/value, or SPENDING money?" If spending, it should be acquisition_announced, offering_announced, or other — never a tradeable contract type. EXCEPTION: stock_buyback is the company spending money to buy its OWN shares — this is BULLISH (reduces float, signals confidence) and should always be classified as stock_buyback, never as a cash outflow.

- government_contract: Company has been NEWLY AWARDED or WON a concrete contract from a US federal/state agency, NASA, DARPA, DoD, or well-known international agency (NATO, UN, major allied nations). Must be a NEW deal the market has not seen before, with the company RECEIVING revenue for delivering products or services. Look for award language: "awarded", "wins", "receives contract", "selected for contract", "secures contract" — but recognize that any principle-equivalent wording also qualifies (see PRINCIPLE-FIRST RULE). Apply the CONTRACT GUARDRAILS (below) before confirming — if it fails ANY of (a)-(n), classify as "other" instead.
- military_contract: Company has been NEWLY AWARDED a contract from a military branch (Army, Navy, Air Force, Space Force, Marines) or for military products (weapons, drones, munitions, counter-UAS, satellites for defense, foreign military sales). Apply CONTRACT GUARDRAILS (below) — must be a NEW award.
- defense_order: Company has received a NEW order from a defense contractor or for defense products (not government directly). Apply CONTRACT GUARDRAILS (below).
- major_contract: A NEW commercial arrangement where the subject company will RECEIVE revenue from a counterparty. The SUBSTANCE is what matters: any arrangement whose economic nature is "company X will now earn money from counterparty Y for products/services" qualifies, regardless of whether the headline calls it a contract, program, order, agreement, rollout, launch at [retailer], supply deal, win, selection, designation as supplier, or any other synonym. The nouns and verbs listed here are common examples, not a closed set — any phrasing describing the same underlying event qualifies. Do NOT require the specific word "contract" — a "program at Target" is the same principle as a "contract with Target".

  MATERIALITY — at least ONE of the following must be present:
    (1) SPECIFIC DOLLAR AMOUNT — even a modest $ figure signals the deal is concrete enough to quantify.
    (2) NAMED MAJOR COUNTERPARTY — use your own judgment of the counterparty's scale and recognition. Household-name companies (large national/global retailers, tech giants, major pharma, top banks, QSR chains, major media, major industrial firms) carry automatic materiality on their name alone — no $ amount needed. Companies that come to mind as household-name anchors include Target, Walmart, Amazon, Costco, Home Depot, Microsoft, Apple, Google, Pfizer, Moderna, McDonald's, Starbucks, JPMorgan — but this is NOT a closed list. Use your pretraining knowledge: any company of comparable recognition and scale qualifies, and you should actively infer beyond the listed anchors. If you recognize the counterparty as a major institution most readers would know, it qualifies. Smaller or unknown counterparties do NOT satisfy this criterion alone — either a $ amount or a "major"/"significant"/"landmark" qualifier (see (4)) must also be present.
    (3) GOVERNMENT/MILITARY ORDER — government entities qualify regardless of dollar amount (use government_contract / military_contract / defense_order instead of major_contract).
    (4) MATERIALITY QUALIFIER — the headline describes the deal as "major", "significant", "landmark", "transformational", "flagship", or equivalent. Self-declared materiality from the subject company is a valid signal because companies deliberately use these words when the deal IS material to them. The qualifier carries weight because the company chose to apply it.

  Any ONE of (1)–(4) is sufficient. A deal with a household-name counterparty is ALWAYS major_contract — no dollar amount needed. A deal self-described as "major" is ALWAYS major_contract — no dollar amount needed. The classic case of a small-cap supplier winning shelf space, supply rights, or a program at a tier-one retailer is ALWAYS major_contract, even with no $ figure.

  EXCLUDE: contract renewals/extensions, PRV sales, pure royalty-only arrangements (ongoing % with no upfront $), M&A, FDA pathway agreements, vague "strategic" partnerships without $ amounts or named major counterparty, acquisitions (company SPENDING money), vague deployments with unnamed customers, and ALL financing/lending arrangements. CRITICAL DISTINCTION: non-dilutive INCOME (contracted revenue the company will EARN) IS major_contract. Non-dilutive CAPITAL (loans, credit facilities, term loans, revolving credit, debt financing, capital from financial firms like Blue Owl, Apollo, Ares, Blackstone lending) is NOT major_contract — classify as debt_restructuring. If the company is BORROWING money or receiving a LOAN/CREDIT LINE, classify as debt_restructuring. If the headline mentions "acquisition of" or "acquires" ANYWHERE, it is NEVER major_contract — use acquisition_announced or acquisition_target.

  Examples that ARE government_contract / military_contract / major_contract (note the variety of wording — the principle is the same):
    "Awarded $50M U.S. Army Contract for Drone Systems" → military_contract (government branch)
    "Wins $15M NASA Contract for Satellite Components" → government_contract
    "Receives $8M DARPA Award for AI Research" → government_contract
    "Wins Major New Fresh-Cut Herb Program at Target Stores" → major_contract (household-name counterparty + 'major' qualifier, no $ needed)
    "Selected as Exclusive Supplier for Walmart Private Label Cosmetics" → major_contract (household-name counterparty + exclusivity as materiality)
    "Launches Nationwide Rollout at Costco Warehouses" → major_contract (household-name counterparty + 'nationwide' as materiality)
    "Signs $20M Deal With Microsoft" → major_contract ($ + named tier-one)
    "Secures $15M Contract With Fortune 500 Bank" → major_contract ($ + recognized institutional counterparty)
    "Chosen by McDonald's to Supply Regional Packaging" → major_contract (household-name counterparty, no $ needed)
    "Announces Landmark Supply Partnership with Starbucks" → major_contract (household-name counterparty + 'landmark' qualifier)
    "Enters $15M Licensing Agreement With Pfizer for Cancer Drug" → major_contract ($ + tier-one pharma + named product)
    "Adds Over $2.6 Million in Contracted, Non-Dilutive Income Through Sublease Agreement" → major_contract ($ amount, contracted income — NOT financing)
  Examples that are NOT (classify as "other" or the noted type):
    "Advances U.S. Homeland Security Contract with Shipment of First PowerBuoy" → other (delivery milestone from existing contract — see guardrail (a))
    "Secures Exclusive Pathway to Offshore Energy Blocks in Liberia" → other (preliminary, developing nation, exploration rights — see guardrails (c), (j), (k))
    "Highlights Strategic Relevance of Maritime Systems Amid Regional Tensions" → other (PR/marketing — see guardrail (f))
    "Extends $10M Army Maintenance Contract" → other (extension, not new — see guardrail (e))
    "Submits Bid for $100M Navy Program" → other (bid, not awarded — see guardrail (d))
    "In Discussions With DoD for Potential Contract" → other (preliminary — see guardrail (c))
    "Ships First Drone System Under Army Contract" → other (delivery milestone — see guardrail (a))
    "Secures New Multifamily Deployment" → other (no named counterparty, no $, no qualifier)
    "RedCloud to Enter Live Deployment Phase as Combined Turkey and Saudi Joint Ventures Represent Up to $80 Million Revenue" → other (deployment phase + revenue projection — see guardrails (a), (n))
    "Receives $1.9 Million First Purchase Order from Luminia" → other (unknown counterparty, no qualifier — see guardrail (m))
    "Announces New Enterprise Partnership With Prominent Company" → other (unnamed counterparty)
    "Secures $500M in Non-Dilutive Capital from Blue Owl" → debt_restructuring (borrowing, not earning)
    "Re-Activates 70% Acquisition of Tekne, Advancing Defense Platform Targeting €100M+ Revenue" → acquisition_announced

CONTRACT GUARDRAILS — APPLY AFTER IDENTIFYING A POTENTIAL CONTRACT:
Once you've identified a headline as potentially qualifying for government_contract, military_contract, defense_order, or major_contract, check these guardrails before confirming. If the headline falls into ANY of these categories, it is NOT a new contract award — classify as "other" (or the noted alternative type). These are safety rails applied AFTER pattern recognition, not skepticism applied first.
  (a) CONTRACT FULFILLMENT/DELIVERY/DEPLOYMENT PHASES: Shipping, delivering, deploying, installing, entering a new phase, or advancing work under an EXISTING contract, joint venture, partnership, or program. The market priced in the deal at announcement time — operational milestones are not new catalysts. ("Advances Contract with Shipment of...", "Ships First Unit Under...", "Delivers on Contract", "Begins Deployment Under Contract", "Completes Installation for...", "First Delivery Under...", "Enters Deployment Phase", "Enters Live Deployment Phase", "Moves to Production Phase", "Begins Commercial Rollout of Joint Venture")
  (b) OPERATIONAL UPDATES: Progress reports, status updates, reaffirmations, or milestones on existing programs. ("Provides Update on Government Program", "Reaches Milestone Under Existing...", "Successfully Tests System for...", "Reaffirms $50M Contract", "Contract Progressing as Expected", "On Track to Deliver Under Contract")
  (c) PRELIMINARY/NON-BINDING: Anything not yet a finalized deal. ("Pathway to...", "Exclusive Pathway", "Framework Agreement", "Letter of Intent", "LOI", "MOU", "Memorandum of Understanding", "Non-binding", "In Discussions With", "In Negotiations With", "Exploring Opportunity", "Preliminary Agreement", "Potential $X Contract", "Potential Deal With...", "Pursuing Contract")
  (d) BIDS/PROPOSALS: Submissions that have not been awarded. ("Submits Bid for...", "Responds to RFP", "Proposes Solution for...", "Shortlisted for...", "Selected to Compete for...")
  (e) EXTENSIONS/RENEWALS/MODIFICATIONS: Not new business. ("Extends Contract", "Renews Agreement", "Contract Extension", "Receives Modification to Existing Contract", "Follow-on Order" without significant expansion)
  (f) PR/MARKETING MENTIONING GOVERNMENT OR COUNTERPARTY: Company talking about relevance without an actual award. ("Highlights Relevance to DoD", "Positioned to Benefit from Government Spending", "Showcases Technology for Military", "Demonstrates Capability at...", "Presents at Government Conference", "Strategic Relevance Amid Tensions")
  (g) PERSONNEL/STAFFING: Hiring or advisory appointments. ("Hires Former DoD Official", "Appoints Military Advisor", "Names Government Relations VP")
  (h) FACILITY/INFRASTRUCTURE: Building or opening facilities, even to support counterparty work. ("Opens Facility to Support DoD", "Expands Manufacturing for Government Programs")
  (i) REVENUE/EARNINGS/BACKLOG FROM CONTRACTS: Accounting recognition or aggregate pipeline metrics. ("Records Revenue from Government Contract", "Government Revenue Up 20%", "Contract Backlog Reaches $500M", "Backlog Grows to $1B", "Order Book Totals $200M")
  (j) FOREIGN DEVELOPING-NATION DEALS: Without a specific dollar amount, government deals from developing nations (Liberia, Nigeria, small island nations, etc.) are NOT credible contract catalysts — classify as "other".
  (k) EXPLORATION/MINING/RESOURCE RIGHTS: Licenses, blocks, concessions, exploration rights = company SPENDING to explore, not receiving revenue.
  (l) CERTIFICATIONS/DESIGNATIONS alone without a contract award: "Added to Approved Vendor List" = "other" at triage level (the sector prompt may still trade these).
  (m) PURCHASE ORDERS FROM UNRECOGNIZED COUNTERPARTIES: A "purchase order" from a non-government, non-military, non-household-name company is NOT government_contract, military_contract, defense_order, or major_contract on its own. Either a $ amount + materiality qualifier must be present, or the counterparty must be a recognized major institution.
  (n) REVENUE PROJECTIONS FROM EXISTING BUSINESS: If the dollar figure describes POTENTIAL or ESTIMATED revenue from existing arrangements (joint ventures, partnerships, programs already in place), it is NOT a new contract — classify as "other". A new contract has a DEFINED VALUE agreed upon at signing ("Awarded $50M contract", "Signs $20M deal"). A revenue projection is speculative ("Represents up to $80M revenue", "Expected to generate $X", "Combined ventures represent $X", "Targeting $X in revenue"). "Up to" and "represents" are red flags — they describe potential, not a concrete award. The dollar amount must be the VALUE OF A NEW DEAL, not a forecast of future revenue from existing business.
- acquisition_target: the company named FIRST in the headline is BEING acquired/bought. Look for: "X to be acquired", "X agrees to be acquired", "X enters agreement to be acquired", "Y to acquire X" (X is target), "tender offer for X", "X receives buyout offer". Also: if headline says "Y and X announce acquisition" and context shows Y is buying X, classify as acquisition_target if X is the smaller/subject company being absorbed.
- acquisition_announced: the company named FIRST in the headline is DOING the acquiring/buying (spending money). Look for: "X acquires Y", "X signs LOI to acquire", "X closes acquisition of", "X to acquire Y", "X secures ownership of", "X re-activates acquisition of", "X completes purchase of", "X secures ownership of". The acquirer is SPENDING cash — this is bearish for the acquirer's stock. IMPORTANT: Even if the headline mentions revenue targets, defense context, dollar amounts, or other positive-sounding details, if the company is ACQUIRING another entity, it is ALWAYS acquisition_announced.
- product_launch: Company launches, deploys, or begins offering a NEW product, service, platform, or business division. REQUIRES: a concrete product/service description AND at least ONE of: (1) specific revenue or transaction figures ("$2M in first two months", "$500K monthly recurring"), (2) named customers or user counts ("500 active merchants", "partnership with [named company]"), (3) specific product capability or function described (not just "new platform" — what does it DO?). The product must be NEW — not an update to an existing product. "Launches" or "Introduces" language is key. This includes tech-enhanced divisions (e.g., "AI-enabled MICE Division with $2M revenue") where the launch + revenue are the catalyst, not the AI component itself.
  IMPORTANT: If the product being launched IS an AI/robotics/ML system and meets ai_breakthrough Path A criteria, classify as ai_breakthrough instead — the AI technology is the core catalyst, not just the launch event.
  Examples that ARE product_launch: "Launches AI-Enabled MICE Division, Achieves Over $2M in First Two Month Transaction Values" (concrete revenue + specific division — AI is secondary to the revenue), "Deploys New SaaS Platform With 200 Enterprise Customers" (named traction), "Introduces Autonomous Delivery Service, Completes 10,000 Deliveries" (concrete results)
  Examples that are NOT product_launch (classify as "other"): "Announces Plans to Launch New Product" (future, not launched), "Updates Platform With New Features" (update, not new product), "Introduces New Service" (no specifics, no revenue, no traction)
  Examples that are ai_breakthrough NOT product_launch: "Launch of Vinci AI Vision System with Delivery of First Humanoid Robot to Ericsson" (AI/robotics IS the product — classify as ai_breakthrough)
- ai_breakthrough: Headlines where AI/robotics/machine-learning is the CORE catalyst — not a buzzword. Must describe a NEW event the market has not seen before. Two paths qualify:
  PATH A — NEW PRODUCT LAUNCH: Company NEWLY launches, deploys, or unveils a specific AI product, tool, agent, or platform for the FIRST TIME. REQUIRES at least TWO of: (1) named AI product/tool/agent, (2) specific function it performs, (3) concrete results claim. The product must be NEW — not an update, milestone, or performance report on an existing AI product.
  PATH B — NEW AI PARTNERSHIP: Company NEWLY announces a collaboration, partnership, or expansion that is SPECIFICALLY about building or deploying AI/robotics/ML capabilities. REQUIRES ALL of: (1) a named partner or counterparty, (2) a concrete AI/robotics domain (e.g. "AI tutoring", "robotics education", "ML drug discovery"), (3) clear growth signal (expand, launch, deploy, build, develop, roll out — NOT just "explore" or "evaluate"). The partnership must be NEWLY ANNOUNCED — not an update on or expansion of an existing AI partnership.
  EXCLUSION — THE FOLLOWING ARE NOT ai_breakthrough (always classify as "other"):
    (1) MILESTONES/METRICS ON EXISTING AI PRODUCTS: Performance updates, usage metrics, transaction volumes, accuracy improvements, or milestones on AI products that were ALREADY LAUNCHED. The market priced in the product at launch time. ("AI Platform Processes 1M Transactions", "AI Agent Achieves Record Accuracy", "AI System Handles 500K Queries", "AI Tool Reaches 10K Users")
    (2) UPDATES/IMPROVEMENTS TO EXISTING AI PRODUCTS: New features, version updates, or enhancements to already-launched AI products are not new launches. ("Updates AI Agent with New Features", "Improves AI Platform Accuracy to 98%", "Releases Version 2.0 of AI Tool", "Enhances AI Capabilities")

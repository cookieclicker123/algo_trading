Rules:

PRIORITY RULE — MERGER vs ACQUISITION (DIFFERENT EVENTS):
A "Merger Agreement" between two companies is NOT an acquisition. In a merger, two companies COMBINE — neither is spending cash to buy the other. This is fundamentally different from "Company A acquires Company B" (cash outflow). Classify correctly:
  merger_agreement: "Announce Merger Agreement", "Enter Into Merger Agreement", "Agree to Merge", "Merger of Equals", "Sign Definitive Merger Agreement", "All-Stock Merger". Two named companies combining.
  acquisition_announced: "Acquires", "To Acquire", "Closes Acquisition", "Signs LOI to Acquire", "Secures Ownership Of", "Completes Purchase Of". One company BUYING another — cash outflow, bearish for acquirer.
  acquisition_target: "To Be Acquired", "Agrees to Be Acquired", "Receives Buyout Offer". Company BEING bought — bullish.
  CRITICAL: "Merger Agreement" → merger_agreement. "Acquires" → acquisition_announced. Do NOT classify mergers as acquisitions.

PRIORITY RULE — ACQUISITION OVERRIDES EVERYTHING:
If the headline contains ANY acquisition language ("acquires", "acquisition of", "to acquire", "re-activates acquisition", "closes acquisition", "signs LOI to acquire", "secures ownership of", "completes purchase of"), it MUST be classified as acquisition_announced or acquisition_target — NEVER as major_contract, government_contract, defense_order, or any other type, regardless of revenue targets, dollar amounts, defense context, or any other details in the headline. An acquisition is a CASH OUTFLOW (company spending money to buy another entity) — it is fundamentally different from a contract (company receiving money for services). Check for acquisition language FIRST before considering any other classification. NOTE: "Merger Agreement" is NOT acquisition — see MERGER vs ACQUISITION rule above.

PRIORITY RULE — BUYBACK vs OFFERING (OPPOSITE EVENTS — DO NOT CONFUSE):
A "self tender offer to purchase shares" or "tender offer to purchase shares" = the company BUYING BACK its own stock = stock_buyback (BULLISH). An "offering" = the company SELLING new shares = offering_announced (BEARISH/dilutive). These are OPPOSITE events. The word "offer" appears in both but they mean completely different things. Check the DIRECTION: is the company PURCHASING/BUYING BACK shares (stock_buyback) or SELLING/ISSUING new shares (offering_announced)?
  stock_buyback: "Self Tender Offer To Purchase Shares", "Commences Tender Offer To Purchase Up To X Shares", "Announces Share Repurchase Program", "Board Authorizes Stock Buyback", "Modified Dutch Auction Tender Offer"
  offering_announced: "Prices Public Offering", "Announces Registered Direct Offering", "Prices Secondary Offering", "Closes Private Placement", "Enters Into Securities Purchase Agreement"
  CRITICAL: If the headline says "Tender Offer To PURCHASE" shares → stock_buyback. If it says "Offering OF shares" or "Prices Offering" → offering_announced. NEVER classify a share repurchase/buyback/tender-offer-to-purchase as offering_announced.
  EXCLUSION — FUND/TRUST TENDER OFFERS ARE NOT stock_buyback:
  stock_buyback ONLY applies to OPERATING COMPANIES (companies that sell products or services) buying back their own COMMON stock. The following are NOT stock_buyback — always classify as "other":
    (1) CLOSED-END FUND / INVESTMENT FUND / TRUST tender offers: If the entity name contains "Fund", "Trust", "Capital Fund", "Income Fund", "Strategy Fund", "Investment Fund", or is clearly a closed-end fund or investment vehicle, its tender offer is routine NAV discount management — NOT a bullish catalyst. These funds regularly do conditional tender offers to narrow their NAV discount. This is housekeeping, not a signal of confidence.
    (2) PREFERRED SHARE tender offers: Tender offers for "preferred shares", "auction preferred shares", or any non-common-stock class are capital structure management — NOT stock_buyback.
    (3) CONDITIONAL tender offers from funds: "Board Determination to Proceed with Conditional Tender Offer" from a fund = "other".
  Examples that are NOT stock_buyback (classify as "other"):
    "Japan Smaller Capitalization Fund Announces Board Determination to Proceed with Conditional Tender Offer" → other (closed-end fund)
    "Virtus Dividend, Interest & Premium Strategy Fund Announces Tender Offer" → other (closed-end fund)
    "Eaton Vance Senior Income Trust Announce Tender Offers for Outstanding Auction Preferred Shares" → other (trust + preferred shares)
    "AllianzGI Convertible & Income Fund Announces Tender Offer" → other (closed-end fund)
    "BlackRock Capital Allocation Trust Commences Tender Offer" → other (investment trust)
  Examples that ARE stock_buyback:
    "Apple Announces $100B Share Repurchase Program" → stock_buyback (operating company, common stock)
    "Tesla Commences Tender Offer To Purchase Up To 10M Shares" → stock_buyback (operating company, common stock)

CASH FLOW DIRECTION PRINCIPLE:
We only trade events where the company RECEIVES value (cash inflow, achievement, approval). Events where the company SPENDS money (acquisitions, investments outward, facility purchases) are cash outflows and bearish. When in doubt about a headline, ask: "Is the company RECEIVING money/value, or SPENDING money?" If spending, it should be acquisition_announced, offering_announced, or other — never a tradeable contract type. EXCEPTION: stock_buyback is the company spending money to buy its OWN shares — this is BULLISH (reduces float, signals confidence) and should always be classified as stock_buyback, never as a cash outflow.

PRIORITY RULE — CONTRACT AUTHENTICITY CHECK:
Before classifying ANY headline as government_contract, military_contract, defense_order, or major_contract, you MUST verify the headline describes a NEW, CONCRETE contract AWARD — meaning the company has NEWLY WON or been AWARDED a specific deal. If the headline merely MENTIONS a government agency, military branch, contract, or deal in ANY other context, classify as "other". A headline that contains the word "contract" is NOT automatically a contract award. Ask: "Has this company JUST NOW been awarded a NEW deal it did not have before?" If no → "other".

THE FOLLOWING ARE NEVER government_contract, military_contract, defense_order, OR major_contract — always classify as "other":
  (a) CONTRACT FULFILLMENT/DELIVERY: Shipping, delivering, deploying, installing, or advancing work under an ALREADY AWARDED contract. The market priced in the contract at award time. ("Advances Contract with Shipment of...", "Ships First Unit Under...", "Delivers on Contract", "Begins Deployment Under Contract", "Completes Installation for...", "First Delivery Under...")
  (b) OPERATIONAL UPDATES: Progress reports, status updates, or milestones on existing programs. ("Provides Update on Government Program", "Reaches Milestone Under Existing...", "Successfully Tests System for...")
  (c) PRELIMINARY/NON-BINDING: Anything not yet a finalized deal. ("Pathway to...", "Exclusive Pathway", "Framework Agreement", "Letter of Intent", "LOI", "MOU", "Memorandum of Understanding", "Non-binding", "In Discussions With", "In Negotiations With", "Exploring Opportunity", "Preliminary Agreement")
  (d) BIDS/PROPOSALS: Submissions that have not been awarded. ("Submits Bid for...", "Responds to RFP", "Proposes Solution for...", "Shortlisted for...", "Selected to Compete for...")
  (e) EXTENSIONS/RENEWALS/MODIFICATIONS: Not new business. ("Extends Contract", "Renews Agreement", "Contract Extension", "Receives Modification to Existing Contract", "Follow-on Order" without significant expansion)
  (f) PR/MARKETING MENTIONING GOVERNMENT: Company talking about government relevance without an actual award. ("Highlights Relevance to DoD", "Positioned to Benefit from Government Spending", "Showcases Technology for Military", "Demonstrates Capability at...", "Presents at Government Conference", "Strategic Relevance Amid Tensions")
  (g) PERSONNEL/STAFFING: Hiring or advisory appointments. ("Hires Former DoD Official", "Appoints Military Advisor", "Names Government Relations VP")
  (h) FACILITY/INFRASTRUCTURE: Building or opening facilities, even to support government work. ("Opens Facility to Support DoD", "Expands Manufacturing for Government Programs")
  (i) REVENUE/EARNINGS FROM CONTRACTS: Accounting recognition. ("Records Revenue from Government Contract", "Government Revenue Up 20%")
  (j) FOREIGN DEVELOPING-NATION DEALS: Without a specific dollar amount, government deals from developing nations (Liberia, Nigeria, small island nations, etc.) are NOT credible contract catalysts — classify as "other".
  (k) EXPLORATION/MINING/RESOURCE RIGHTS: Licenses, blocks, concessions, exploration rights = company SPENDING to explore, not receiving revenue.
  (l) CERTIFICATIONS/DESIGNATIONS alone without a contract award: "Added to Approved Vendor List" = "other" at triage level (the sector prompt may still trade these).
  (m) PURCHASE ORDERS FROM PRIVATE/UNKNOWN COMPANIES: A "purchase order" from a non-government, non-military, non-Fortune 500 company is NOT government_contract, military_contract, or defense_order. If the counterparty is not a recognized government agency, military branch, or well-known major institution, classify as "other". "Receives Purchase Order from [Unknown Company]" = "other". Only classify as major_contract if $ amount AND well-known counterparty are both present.

- government_contract: Company has been NEWLY AWARDED or WON a concrete contract from a US federal/state agency, NASA, DARPA, DoD, or well-known international agency (NATO, UN, major allied nations). Must be a NEW deal the market has not seen before, with the company RECEIVING revenue for delivering products or services. Look for award language: "awarded", "wins", "receives contract", "selected for contract", "secures contract". Verify it passes the CONTRACT AUTHENTICITY CHECK above — if it fails ANY of (a)-(l), classify as "other" instead.
- military_contract: Company has been NEWLY AWARDED a contract from a military branch (Army, Navy, Air Force, Space Force, Marines) or for military products (weapons, drones, munitions, counter-UAS, satellites for defense, foreign military sales). Same CONTRACT AUTHENTICITY CHECK applies — must be a NEW award.
- defense_order: Company has received a NEW order from a defense contractor or for defense products (not government directly). Same CONTRACT AUTHENTICITY CHECK applies.
- major_contract: NEW commercial contracts/orders/agreements that are CONCRETE and MATERIAL. Same CONTRACT AUTHENTICITY CHECK (a)-(l) applies. Additionally REQUIRES at least ONE of: (1) specific dollar amount, (2) named well-known counterparty (Fortune 500, major institution, government entity), (3) government/military order. Must be revenue-generating for the company (company RECEIVING money for products/services delivered). This includes sublease agreements, service contracts, supply agreements, and any other arrangement where the company receives contracted income with a specific dollar amount. EXCLUDE: contract renewals/extensions, PRV sales, pure royalty-only arrangements (ongoing % of sales without concrete upfront dollar amounts), merger/acquisition agreements, FDA pathway agreements, vague "strategic" partnerships without $ amounts, company SPENDING money (acquisitions, investments outward), vague deployments/partnerships with unnamed customers, and ALL financing/lending/capital arrangements — non-dilutive CAPITAL (loans, credit facilities, term loans, revolving credit, debt financing, capital raises from financial firms e.g. Blue Owl, Apollo, Ares, Blackstone lending), share repurchase program announcements. CRITICAL DISTINCTION: "non-dilutive income" or "contracted income" (company EARNING revenue) is NOT the same as "non-dilutive capital" (company BORROWING money). Non-dilutive income with a dollar amount = major_contract. Non-dilutive capital/financing = debt_restructuring. KEY: if the headline has NO dollar amount AND NO named counterparty AND is NOT a government order, it is NOT major_contract — classify as "other". KEY: if the company is BORROWING money or receiving a LOAN/CREDIT LINE (even with a large $ amount and named lender), it is NOT major_contract — classify as "debt_restructuring" or "stock_buyback" as appropriate. KEY: if the headline mentions "acquisition of" or "acquires" ANYWHERE, it is NEVER major_contract — always use acquisition_announced or acquisition_target (see PRIORITY RULE above).
  Examples that ARE government_contract/military_contract/major_contract:
    "Awarded $50M U.S. Army Contract for Drone Systems" → military_contract
    "Wins $15M NASA Contract for Satellite Components" → government_contract
    "Receives $8M DARPA Award for AI Research" → government_contract
    "Secures $15M Contract With Fortune 500" → major_contract
    "Signs $20M Deal With Microsoft" → major_contract
    "Enters $15M Licensing Agreement With Pfizer for Cancer Drug" → major_contract (material licensing deal with dollar amount + named counterparty + named product)
    "Adds Over $2.6 Million in Contracted, Non-Dilutive Income Through Sublease Agreement" → major_contract (contracted income with specific dollar amount — NOT financing/capital)
  Examples that are NOT (classify as "other"):
    "Advances U.S. Homeland Security Contract with Shipment of First PowerBuoy" → other (delivery milestone from existing contract)
    "Secures Exclusive Pathway to Offshore Energy Blocks in Liberia" → other (preliminary, developing nation, exploration rights)
    "Highlights Strategic Relevance of Maritime Systems Amid Regional Tensions" → other (PR/marketing)
    "Extends $10M Army Maintenance Contract" → other (extension, not new)
    "Submits Bid for $100M Navy Program" → other (bid, not awarded)
    "In Discussions With DoD for Potential Contract" → other (preliminary)
    "Ships First Drone System Under Army Contract" → other (delivery milestone)
    "Secures New Multifamily Deployment" → other (no $ amount, no named customer)
    "Receives $1.9 Million First Purchase Order from Luminia" → other (purchase order from unknown private company, not government/military/Fortune 500)
    "Announces New Enterprise Partnership With Prominent Company" → other (unnamed)
    "Secures $500M in Non-Dilutive Capital from Blue Owl" → debt_restructuring (financing)
    "Re-Activates 70% Acquisition of Tekne, Advancing Defense Platform Targeting €100M+ Revenue" → acquisition_announced
- acquisition_target: the company named FIRST in the headline is BEING acquired/bought. Look for: "X to be acquired", "X agrees to be acquired", "X enters agreement to be acquired", "Y to acquire X" (X is target), "tender offer for X", "X receives buyout offer". Also: if headline says "Y and X announce acquisition" and context shows Y is buying X, classify as acquisition_target if X is the smaller/subject company being absorbed.
- acquisition_announced: the company named FIRST in the headline is DOING the acquiring/buying (spending money). Look for: "X acquires Y", "X signs LOI to acquire", "X closes acquisition of", "X to acquire Y", "X secures ownership of", "X re-activates acquisition of", "X completes purchase of", "X secures ownership of". The acquirer is SPENDING cash — this is bearish for the acquirer's stock. IMPORTANT: Even if the headline mentions revenue targets, defense context, dollar amounts, or other positive-sounding details, if the company is ACQUIRING another entity, it is ALWAYS acquisition_announced.
- product_launch: Company launches, deploys, or begins offering a NEW product, service, platform, or business division. REQUIRES: a concrete product/service description AND at least ONE of: (1) specific revenue or transaction figures ("$2M in first two months", "$500K monthly recurring"), (2) named customers or user counts ("500 active merchants", "partnership with [named company]"), (3) specific product capability or function described (not just "new platform" — what does it DO?). The product must be NEW — not an update to an existing product. "Launches" or "Introduces" language is key. This includes tech-enhanced divisions (e.g., "AI-enabled MICE Division with $2M revenue") where the launch + revenue are the catalyst, not the AI component itself.
  IMPORTANT: If the product being launched IS an AI/robotics/ML system and meets ai_breakthrough Path A criteria, classify as ai_breakthrough instead — the AI technology is the core catalyst, not just the launch event.
  Examples that ARE product_launch: "Launches AI-Enabled MICE Division, Achieves Over $2M in First Two Month Transaction Values" (concrete revenue + specific division — AI is secondary to the revenue), "Deploys New SaaS Platform With 200 Enterprise Customers" (named traction), "Introduces Autonomous Delivery Service, Completes 10,000 Deliveries" (concrete results)
  Examples that are NOT product_launch (classify as "other"): "Announces Plans to Launch New Product" (future, not launched), "Updates Platform With New Features" (update, not new product), "Introduces New Service" (no specifics, no revenue, no traction)
  Examples that are ai_breakthrough NOT product_launch: "Launch of Vinci AI Vision System with Delivery of First Humanoid Robot to Ericsson" (AI/robotics IS the product — classify as ai_breakthrough)
- ai_breakthrough: Headlines where AI/robotics/machine-learning is the CORE catalyst — not a buzzword. Two paths qualify:
  PATH A — PRODUCT LAUNCH: Company launches, deploys, or unveils a specific AI product, tool, agent, or platform. REQUIRES at least TWO of: (1) named AI product/tool/agent, (2) specific function it performs, (3) concrete results claim.
  PATH B — AI EXPANSION: Company announces a collaboration, partnership, or expansion that is SPECIFICALLY about building or deploying AI/robotics/ML capabilities. REQUIRES ALL of: (1) a named partner or counterparty, (2) a concrete AI/robotics domain (e.g. "AI tutoring", "robotics education", "ML drug discovery"), (3) clear growth signal (expand, launch, deploy, build, develop, roll out — NOT just "explore" or "evaluate").
  Examples that ARE ai_breakthrough: "Launches AI Agent Revolutionizing Biospecimen Matching" (Path A), "Deploys AI Drug Discovery Platform Achieving 95% Accuracy" (Path A), "Launch of Vinci AI Vision System with Delivery of First Humanoid Robot to Ericsson" (Path A — named AI product + robotics function + delivery to named customer), "Announces Collaboration With Walimaker to Expand AI and Robotics Learning Programs" (Path B — named partner + concrete AI domain + expansion), "Partners With NVIDIA to Build AI-Powered Medical Imaging Pipeline" (Path B), "Begins Collaboration With Tencent to Support AI Tutor Platform With Real-Time Voice" (Path B)
  Examples that are NOT ai_breakthrough (classify as "other" or "partnership"): "Announces AI-Powered Solutions" (no specifics), "Integrating AI Into Platform" (vague), "AI-Driven Revenue Growth" (marketing), "Partners With AI Company" (no domain specifics, unnamed capabilities), "Hiring Chief AI Officer" (staffing), "Exploring AI Opportunities" (no commitment), "Leveraging AI To Enhance Operations" (buzzword)
- ai_rebranding: Company formally rebrands, renames, or transforms its corporate identity to signal a strategic pivot into AI, technology, or a high-growth sector. The headline must describe a COMPLETED rebrand or name change — not a plan, not a product launch, not "exploring AI." The market treats these as transformational identity shifts regardless of underlying fundamentals.
  REQUIRES: (1) explicit rebrand/rename/name-change language ("rebrands as", "changes name to", "renames to", "now operating as"), AND (2) the new identity clearly signals a pivot to AI, technology, blockchain, quantum, or similar high-growth theme.
  MUST NOT be ai_rebranding if: company just adds AI features to existing products (ai_breakthrough or other), mentions AI in a partnership/product context without renaming (use appropriate type), or announces plans to rebrand in the future (other).
  Examples that ARE ai_rebranding: "Myseum Rebrands as Myseum.AI to Align with Full Suite of Technology and Social Media Platforms", "XYZ Corp Changes Name to XYZ.AI, Announces Strategic Pivot to Artificial Intelligence", "Company Completes Corporate Rebrand to Reflect AI-First Strategy"
  Examples that are NOT ai_rebranding: "Announces AI Strategy" (no rebrand), "Launches AI Division" (product_launch or ai_breakthrough), "Plans to Rename Company" (future, not completed), "Adds .AI to Product Line" (product, not corporate rebrand)
- clinical_breakthrough: Phase 2 or later clinical trial with EXCEPTIONAL positive results for a MAJOR, WIDELY-KNOWN disease. REQUIRES ALL of:
  (1) Phase 2 or Phase 3 (NOT Phase 1 — too early, safety-only)
  (2) Concrete positive outcome: met primary endpoint, statistically significant results, superiority over standard of care, OR strong response rates with specific numbers
  (3) Disease MUST be on this list OR be an equally famous, widely-feared disease you are ABSOLUTELY CERTAIN would spark the same investor reaction: cancer (lung, breast, colorectal, prostate, pancreatic, liver, ovarian, brain/glioblastoma, melanoma, leukemia, lymphoma), Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, ALS, obesity/GLP-1, diabetes, NASH/MASH, heart failure, MS, lupus, rheumatoid arthritis, sickle cell, muscular dystrophy, epinephrine delivery, anaphylaxis
  CRITICAL: The bar is "household name disease that the general public knows and fears." If you have to think about whether it qualifies, it doesn't — use clinical_trial_positive. Diseases that do NOT qualify include but are not limited to: back pain, lumbar disc disease, chronic pain, arthritis (non-RA), COPD, asthma, eczema, psoriasis, IBS, Crohn's, ulcerative colitis, kidney disease, liver fibrosis, eye diseases, hearing loss, rare/orphan diseases, genetic disorders not listed. These may still be clinical_trial_positive — just not clinical_breakthrough.
  MUST NOT be clinical_breakthrough if: Phase 1, safety/tolerability only, extension study, preclinical/in vitro, "potential"/"may"/"could", trial initiation with no results, vague "positive trends" without specifics, disease not on the exact list above
  Examples that ARE clinical_breakthrough: "Phase 2 Study Shows Statistically Significant Tumor Reduction in Colorectal Cancer", "Phase 3 Trial Meets Primary Endpoint in Alzheimer's", "Phase 2 Achieves Superior Epinephrine Delivery vs EpiPen"
  Examples that are NOT (use clinical_trial_positive): "Phase 1 Safety Data Shows Favorable Profile", "Phase 2 Shows Encouraging Trends", "Positive Data in Rare Enzyme Deficiency" (niche), "Initiates Phase 3 Trial" (no results), "Phase 2 Demonstrates Meaningful Improvements in Chronic Lumbar Disc Disease" (not on disease list), "Phase 3 Meets Endpoint in COPD" (not on disease list)
- cancer_catalyst: Cancer/oncology headline with concrete positive developments and named specifics, but does NOT meet the stricter clinical_breakthrough criteria (Phase 2+ met primary endpoint). REQUIRES ALL of: (1) Cancer or specific oncology disease named (lung, breast, colorectal, prostate, pancreatic, liver, ovarian, glioblastoma, melanoma, leukemia, lymphoma, etc.), (2) Named drug, compound, therapy, or specific mechanism (checkpoint inhibitor, ADC, CAR-T, etc.), (3) Concrete positive development: patent granted/approved, positive clinical data (any phase with specifics), novel mechanism partnership with named partner, regulatory milestone. MUST NOT be cancer_catalyst if: trial initiation with no results, preclinical/in vitro, "potential"/"may"/"could" without concrete outcome, safety/tolerability only, vague "cancer research" without named drug or specifics. If headline qualifies for clinical_breakthrough, use clinical_breakthrough instead (higher priority).
  Examples that ARE cancer_catalyst: "AIM ImmunoTech Granted New Patent for Ampligen Use in Pancreatic Cancer Treatment", "CytomX Therapeutics Reports Positive Phase 1b Data for CX-904 ADC in Solid Tumors With Named Tumor Types"
  Examples that are NOT cancer_catalyst (classify as clinical_trial_positive or other): "Initiates Phase 1 Cancer Trial" (no results), "Potential Cancer Treatment Shows Promise" (vague), "Preclinical Data Shows Anti-Tumor Activity" (preclinical), "Announces Cancer Research Collaboration" (no named drug or specifics)
- If unsure between military_contract and government_contract, prefer military_contract for anything involving armed forces, weapons, or defense systems
- partnership: A NEW strategic partnership, collaboration, or alliance between TWO NAMED companies with CONCRETE scope. REQUIRES: (1) both parties named (not "major partner" or "leading company"), AND (2) at least ONE of: specific dollar amount, specific product/service/technology named, specific market/application described. Vague partnerships without specifics = "other". If the partnership is specifically about AI/robotics/ML and meets ai_breakthrough PATH B criteria, use ai_breakthrough instead. If the partnership involves a specific dollar contract value, consider major_contract instead.
  Examples that ARE partnership: "Partners With Microsoft to Deploy Cloud Analytics Platform" (named partner + specific product), "Enters Strategic Alliance With Pfizer for Drug Co-Development" (named + specific scope)
  Examples that are NOT partnership (classify as "other"): "Announces Strategic Partnership" (unnamed partner), "Partners With Leading Company for Innovation" (no specifics), "Signs Partnership Agreement" (no details)
- licensing_agreement: A NEW licensing deal where the company RECEIVES license revenue or gains rights to specific named technology/IP. REQUIRES: named counterparty OR specific dollar amount OR named technology/IP. Vague "licensing arrangements" = "other".
  PRIORITY ROUTING TO major_contract: If the licensing deal includes a SPECIFIC DOLLAR AMOUNT (upfront payment, deal value, or total contract value — not just "royalties" or "milestone payments") AND at least ONE of: (1) named counterparty, (2) named product/technology/IP, (3) specific addressable market size — classify as major_contract instead. The dollar amount makes it functionally a major contract regardless of legal structure. The more named entities and concrete figures in the headline, the stronger the case for major_contract.
  Examples that route to major_contract: "Enters $15M Licensing Agreement With Pfizer for Cancer Drug Platform" → major_contract (dollar amount + named counterparty + named product), "Signs $50M License Deal for Proprietary Battery Technology in $2B EV Market" → major_contract (dollar amount + named technology + market size), "Licenses Diabetes Drug to Novo Nordisk for $30M Upfront Plus Milestones" → major_contract (dollar amount + named counterparty + named product)
  Examples that stay licensing_agreement: "Receives Technology License from Qualcomm" → licensing_agreement (no dollar amount), "Enters Licensing Arrangement for Undisclosed Terms" → other (vague), "Licenses Drug Compound to Roche for Royalties" → licensing_agreement (no upfront dollar amount)
- fda_approval: FDA grants FULL APPROVAL (not clearance) for a drug, biologic, or medical product. Look for: "FDA approves", "receives FDA approval", "granted FDA approval". This is the strongest regulatory catalyst.
- fda_clearance: FDA grants 510(k) clearance or De Novo clearance for a medical device. Look for: "FDA clears", "receives 510(k) clearance", "granted FDA clearance". Different from approval — clearance is for devices, approval is for drugs.
- regulatory_approval: Non-FDA regulatory approvals from other agencies (EMA, Health Canada, PMDA, CE marking, DEA scheduling, USDA). Also covers significant government permits, licenses, or designations that unlock new revenue. NOT routine compliance certifications (SOC2, ISO, FedRAMP alone).
  Examples: "Receives EMA Approval for Cancer Drug" → regulatory_approval, "Granted DEA Schedule II Manufacturing License" → regulatory_approval
  NOT regulatory_approval: "Achieves SOC2 Compliance" → other, "Receives ISO Certification" → other
- grant_funding: Company receives a NEW grant, award, or non-dilutive funding from a government agency, foundation, or research body. REQUIRES: specific dollar amount OR named granting body. The key distinction from government_contract is that grants fund RESEARCH/DEVELOPMENT (no deliverable), while contracts fund PRODUCTS/SERVICES (specific deliverable). If unsure, prefer government_contract for awards with specific deliverables.
  Examples: "Awarded $5M NIH Grant for Alzheimer's Research" → grant_funding, "Receives $2M BARDA Funding for Vaccine Development" → grant_funding
  NOT grant_funding: "Wins $10M Army Contract for Drone Delivery" → government_contract (specific deliverable)
- patent_granted: Company is GRANTED or ISSUED a patent (not filed, not pending, not applied for). Must be a final grant/issuance. "Patent allowance" alone = "other" (not final). "Files patent" = "other". Only the actual grant matters.
  Examples: "Granted U.S. Patent for Novel Drug Delivery System" → patent_granted, "Issued Patent Covering AI-Based Diagnostic Method" → patent_granted
  NOT patent_granted: "Files Patent Application" → other, "Receives Patent Allowance" → other (not final grant)
- facility_expansion: Company opens, builds, or significantly expands a physical facility (factory, warehouse, data center, lab). This is an operational milestone — NOT a contract. Classify here when the headline is about the facility itself, not about a contract to build it.
- store_expansion: Retailer opens new stores or enters new geographic markets with physical locations.
- drill_results_positive: Mining or oil/gas company reports positive drilling, assay, or exploration results. Look for: grade/thickness of mineralization, flow rates, resource estimates. "Intersects 50m of 2.5 g/t gold" = drill_results_positive. Vague "exploration update" without results = other.
- clinical_trial_positive: Any clinical trial with positive results that does NOT meet the stricter clinical_breakthrough criteria. Includes: Phase 1 with favorable safety, Phase 2 in non-major diseases, positive data without meeting primary endpoint, any encouraging clinical data.
- clinical_trial_negative: Clinical trial with negative, disappointing, or failed results. Look for: "fails to meet endpoint", "did not achieve", "discontinued", "negative results", "safety concerns". This is a SKIP type — the postfilter blocks it.
- earnings_beat: Company reports earnings/revenue that BEAT analyst estimates. Look for: "beats", "tops estimates", "exceeds expectations", "better than expected".
